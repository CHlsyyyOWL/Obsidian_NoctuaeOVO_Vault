Zhejiang's own provincial regulation, published six months before the national NDRC Order No. 36. This timing is analytically significant because it means Zhejiang was not implementing the central framework but legislating ahead of it. This document provides the core material for coding Zhejiang on all four comparison dimensions.

**Liaoning** Province market supervision data (2025). Reported in Sina Finance, January 2026. Available at: [http://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-01-11/doc-inhfwpxy8866050.shtml](http://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-01-11/doc-inhfwpxy8866050.shtml)

Liaoning completed credit repair for 65,500 business entities in 2025 through market supervision departments. Under the new SAMR regulation, Liaoning compressed the minimum publicity period for general administrative penalties from six months to three months and reduced processing times from 15 to 7 working days. Liaoning also introduced targeted assistance for individual enterprises but did not issue a provincial level regulation. This contrast with Zhejiang, which has its own legislation, is useful for testing H1 and H2 because Liaoning is a province dominated by state owned enterprises and relies on central frameworks without developing its own procedural specifications.

- Zhao, H. and Liu, T. (2024). China's Social Credit System and the Family: Punishment and Collective Resistance. Economy and Society, 54(1), 49-69.
	- Examines how credit system penalties extend beyond the individual to family members and how this generates collective resistance. Relevant to the paper's argument about the legitimacy crisis created by mass blacklisting during the 2020 to 2024 enforcement phase. Provides evidence that punitive overreach was not only an economic problem (blocked market participation) but also a social one (family level consequences), strengthening the case for why repair mechanisms became politically necessary.

	Argues that corporate compliance in China is a product of party state capitalism where the CCP Central Committee and State Council set compliance priorities. Credit repair can be understood as part of this broader compliance regime where the state defines what constitutes acceptable corporate behavior and controls the pathway back to good standing. 5. Pearson, M., Rithmire, M. and Tsai, K. (2022). The State and Capitalism in China. Cambridge Elements. Available at: https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/state-and-capitalism-in-china/621573281A601FF77F085386336A991D
	Introduces the concept of "party state capitalism" where the state prioritizes risk management over growth maximization. 	Finds that citizens accept the SCS but do not actively engage with it. ### Methodology

1. **Ma, Y. (2024). Reconceptualizing Policy Change in China: From Soft to Harder Forms of Law in the Household Registration System Reform. Journal of Chinese Governance,** 9(1), 23-48. Available at: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/23812346.2023.2300179
	The conceptualization of policy change has been much discussed in democratic countries with a focus on formal legislation. However, in non-Western contexts, policies change in both formal and informal ways, and these informal mechanisms are challenging to observe. To fill this gap, **this article uses the case of China’s policy process, where soft law policies play an increasingly important role in driving policy change.** Drawing inspiration from **legalization theory in international law, t**he article creates a novel typology of soft law policies based on dimensions of obligation and enforcement. Next, **it compiles and analyzes a unique dataset of soft law policies from the central, provincial, and prefectural governments during the process** of Household Registration (Hukou) System reform from 2011 to 2021. The results show that when the central government strengthens the obligation or enforcement dimension of the Hukou reform policy, then provincial and prefectural governments largely follow this policy change. Still, significant variations exist across provinces and prefectures in how they adapt to central policies. The findings highlight the potential of the typology of soft law policies to generate new insights into policy changes across China’s multi-level governance system.

## Claude 
H1 (Development Advantages): 人均GDP更高且digital governance infrastructure更完善的省份会制定更清晰的procedural guidelines for credit history restoration，反映更强的institutional capacity。文献对应 Drinhausen & Brussee, Liu & Rona-Tas。

H2 (Private Sector Concentration): private enterprises in trade sectors集中度更高的省份会提供更有利的repair terms和更短的minimum publication periods，因为企业的credit ratings直接影响export financing和procurement contracts的准入。文献对应 Köncke et al.。

H3 (Preservation of Political Influence): 2020到2024年SCS enforcement最活跃的省份会在restoration mechanisms中保留更大的administrative discretion for sectoral regulators，符合administrative subcontracting的逻辑，即local officials将sanctioning and recovery powers视为regulatory capital。文献对应 Creemers, Birley。

#### Methodology

Ma (2024) 在democratic countries关于policy change的conceptualisation主要关注formal legislation，但在non-Western contexts中policies通过formal和informal两种方式变化，这些informal mechanisms难以observe。为填补这一gap，Ma借鉴international law中的legalization theory，创建了基于obligation和enforcement两个dimensions的soft law policies typology，并编译分析了central, provincial和prefectural governments在2011到2021年Hukou reform过程中的soft law policy dataset。结果显示，当central government strengthens obligation或enforcement dimension时，provincial和prefectural governments largely follow，但provinces和prefectures之间在如何adapt to central policies上存在significant variations。 Ma, Y. (2024). Reconceptualizing Policy Change in China: From Soft to Harder Forms of Law in the Household Registration System Reform. _Journal of Chinese Governance_, 9(1), 23-48.

Yin (2025) 在city level比较了Rongcheng和Danyang两地的SCS implementation，使用publicly available government data和machine learning。发现不同的institutional contexts (formal rules, institutional actors, shared beliefs)在同一national framework下产生不同的implementation outcomes。这篇直接支持本文comparing provincial implementation的approach，也为using publicly available policy documents as data提供了methodological reference。Three-level institutional analysis (formal rules, institutional actors, shared beliefs) 可以structure本文的provincial comparison。 Yin, M. (2025). Unlocking Policy Implementation Logic: Comparative Analysis of Chinese Social Credit Systems in Rongcheng and Danyang with an Enterprise Focus. _Chinese Sociological Review_.

#### Institutional Context

Habich-Sobiegalla, Zheng, and Pluemmer (2026) 分析了digital platforms如何restructure citizens和local government之间的accountability relationships。"Temporal governance"这一concept (processing timelines如何成为bureaucratic control的instruments)与本文分析credit repair timelines作为administrative tools的功能相连。核心发现是digital platforms增强了对superiors的upward accountability但没有增强对citizens的horizontal accountability，这与Credit China centralises data但不constraining local discretion的模式parallel。 Habich-Sobiegalla, S., Zheng, H. and Pluemmer, F. (2026). Temporal Governance and Accountability Costs of Beijing's Digital Citizen Request System. _Regulation and Governance_, 20(1), 18-34.
