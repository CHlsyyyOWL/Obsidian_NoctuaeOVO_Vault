# Pre-reading

> [!note]- chat pre-reading
> Good, let’s unpack this carefully. I’ll first summarize Nozick’s view of liberalism, then contrast it with Rawls’s view, and finally highlight the main points of divergence.
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 1. Nozick’s View of Liberalism
> 	•	Core principle: Individual rights are side-constraints. The state may not violate them, even for the sake of social welfare.
> 	•	Entitlement theory of justice: Justice in holdings depends only on how property was acquired and transferred:
> 	1.	Justice in acquisition (initial appropriation).
> 	2.	Justice in transfer (voluntary exchange).
> 	3.	Rectification of injustice (correcting past wrongs).
> 	•	Minimal state: The only justifiable state is one that protects against force, theft, fraud, and enforces contracts. Anything more (e.g., redistribution, welfare) is an illegitimate infringement on liberty.
> 	•	Libertarian liberalism: Strong emphasis on negative liberty (freedom from interference). Redistribution is seen as a violation of self-ownership, comparable to forced labor (since it takes the product of one’s work without consent).
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 2. Rawls’s View of Liberalism
> 	•	Core principle: Justice as fairness. Institutions should be arranged to ensure fairness for all, especially the least advantaged.
> 	•	Two principles of justice (simplified):
> 	1.	Equal basic liberties for all citizens.
> 	2.	Social and economic inequalities are justified only if they:
> a. Benefit the least advantaged (the difference principle), and
> b. Are attached to positions open to all under fair equality of opportunity.
> 	•	Original position & veil of ignorance: A thought experiment to derive principles of justice. Individuals, unaware of their own status, would rationally choose rules that protect against worst-case disadvantage.
> 	•	Redistributive liberalism: Inequalities can exist, but redistribution is justified if it improves the position of the least well-off.
> 
> The contrast is foundational:
> 	•	Rawls is building a theory of fairness, focused on outcomes (a patterned distribution, even if not equal).
> 	•	Nozick is building a theory of rights, focused on process (just acquisition and transfer, regardless of outcome).
> 
> This makes Rawls’s liberalism egalitarian and institutionally active, while Nozick’s liberalism is libertarian and strictly limited in state function.
>

## Compare with Rawls
|                              | **Rawls**                                                                                             | **Nozick**                                                            |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Philosophical Foundation** | Contractarian (social contract under the veil of ignorance).                                          | Natural rights tradition (Lockean self-ownership).                    |
| **State’s Role**             | Active<br>Ensures fair equality of opportunity and redistributes to satisfy the difference principle. | Minimal<br>Protects rights, enforces contracts, prevents force/fraud. |
| **View of Liberty**          | Balances liberty with fairness Redistribution acceptable if consented under fair rules.               | Absolute negative liberty<br>Redistribution is coercion.              |
| **Justice in Distribution**  | distributive justice (difference principle, equal opportunity)<br>                                    | entitlement  justice (acquisition & transfer)                         |
| **Inequality**               | Justified if it benefits the least well-off.                                                          | Irrelevant, so long as property was justly acquired and transferred.  |
| **Redistribution**           | Essential for fairness.                                                                               | Equivalent to forced labor; unjust.                                   |
