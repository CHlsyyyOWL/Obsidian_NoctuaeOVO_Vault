## - What is critical in political theory?
### - Origins of critique

- Kant 康德 - Transcedent Critique
	- What is the meaning of "our present"? In what time do we "live"
	- The use of reason: Enlightenment as the way out of immaturity
	- Immaturity: the inability to use one’s own reason without guidance from another. (have the courage to use your own understanding)
- Foucault - genealogy critique
	- - "*What is Enlightenment?*" - 1984, a response to Kant
	- Modern attitude / critical attitude 
		- Critique is not simply applying universal Reason but **adopting a historical and critical relation to the present**.
        - Enlightenment is not a completed project, but an ongoing ethos — a way of relating to contemporary reality.

> [!note]- Foucault
> . Immanent contradiction critique（内在矛盾批判）
> 	•	代表：马克思、法兰克福学派
> 	•	逻辑：在社会秩序内部寻找矛盾 → 比如资本主义宣称理性/自由，但现实是异化/压迫。
> 	•	目标：揭示内在张力，让社会向其“未竟的承诺”靠拢。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 2. Foucault 的批判方式
> 	•	福柯自己不用“内在矛盾”的语言，而是 genealogy（谱系学）：
> 	•	不假设一个普遍理性或人类潜能作为参照。
> 	•	追踪知识-权力体系如何在历史中偶然生成（例如监狱、精神病学、性科学）。
> 	•	批判的目的不是“让社会回到它真正的理性”，而是 去自然化（denaturalize），显示一切制度与真理话语的偶然性。
> 	•	他称批判是一种 “态度”（attitude） 或 “ethos”（见《什么是启蒙？》）：即不断质疑我们当下的真理游戏。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 3. 所以福柯是“contradiction critique”吗？
> 	•	严格来说：不是。
> 	•	因为“内在矛盾批判”假设社会秩序内部有一个“理性与现实的张力”可被揭示（这背后还有康德—马克思的传统）。
> 	•	福柯不承认有这样的“普遍理性”或“未竟承诺”。
> 	•	他的批判是 谱系式的、去自然化的、后形而上学的。
> 

### - What is critique theory?

- **Frankfurt 
	- Most of them were influenced by Marxism
	- Witnessed the failure of revolutions in the West and the rise of Fascism, admitting the latter a challenge to Marxism
	- Marxism is a philosophy of history, saying that the communist revolution is inevitable 
	- However, they found the middle-class does not naturally support communist parties, establishing theories to understand the drag that is why the middle class votes for Fascism, which is against their own interests. 
- Two main dimensions: 
- Horkheimer 霍克海默 - "*Traditional and Critical Theory*": comparing critical theory in contrast to traditional theory
	- **Theoretical aspects**: not only descriptive but also normative ( different from what it is and what it could be)
		- Traditional theory: the common way of modern research, which views the world as something objective 
	- **Critical theory**: the contemporary world is really **IRRATIONAL**
		- Viewing the world is non-objective; the world is **contingent and transformable**, what is called "social order", not only an inquiry into what it is, but **something it ought to be**. 
		- **Social Relation of Dominance**: Social relation is shaped by identity and relations of dominance. Hence, the world cannot be objectively described and needs to be adopted by socially dominated groups. 
		- **Epistemological View** (认识论): Critical theory holds that knowledge is not a neutral reflection of reality, but is constituted within and **by structures of power.** Critique exposes the **internal tensions** of social order as the basis for critique and transformation。
		- **Social Reality is Conflictual**: Argues that the world is a product of social practice, which means the society is also **conflictual** - recall Agonism - should base your norms of critique under the critique in the sense that the world in some way is IRRATIONAL.

## - What is the history of political theory?
### - Ancient political philosophy

Two main dimensions of Ancient political philosophy
- A political community is natural
- The political sphere is an ethical sphere, directed to the request for good (good life). Its normative nature is the search for the Good.

Plato, The Republic (Politeia) 375 BC
- Critique of Democracy
- Aim: to define justice and organisation of a just city (normative)
- The world is cosmological: Truth and good are the essence and are not subject to change. The world aims to achieve such an essence. 
- Harmony between cosmological, political, and psychological orders. The city is divided into 3 parts:
	- Philosophers (reason)
	- warriors (courage)
	- workers (appetite)
- Philosophers-Kings 哲人王: response to the trauma of Socrates' death in Athenian democracy
	- Plato 柏拉图
		- to build a just city 正义城邦
		- Democracy is a threat to philosophers, as people cannot understand philosophy, and ultimately, philosophy will be rejected. So it is to protect philosophy to reject democracy. 
	- Hannah Arendt (in *The Human Condition*) 
		- The idea of Philosophers-King is not a true political aim, but a strategy to protect philosophers, to build a shelter for philosopher people. 
		- Critique PK: It opens the tradition of Anti-philosophy, that politics seeing philosophy as a secondary enemy. 
	- Aristotle
		- Aristotle does not refer to PK; instead, saying the political community is the natural environment for human beings.
		- **The autonomy of politic**s: “A good philosopher has wisdom(sophia), while a politician has to have practical intelligence (Phronesis). Politics cannot simply be subordinated to philosophy, because it requires its own mode of reasoning and judgment rooted in action, plurality, and contingency.
		- Aristotle does not want to make Utopia, and he is descriptive, wanting to describe how you can improve based on the real base of the actual constitution.

### A New Conception of freedom

**- Isaiah Berlin: ”Two concepts of liberty” 
- PM = transition from positive conception of freedom to a negative one (see also Constant ”The liberty of Ancients compared with that of the Moderns”) 

- Positive Freedom: “wish on the part of the individual to be his own master”
	- Individual autonomy -> use of reason / search for a “true” self (normative vision of the “self”) 
	- Collective autonomy -> self-realization through political participation
	- Modern incarnation: Republicanism 
	- 
- Negative Freedom: “free to the degree to which no man or body of men interferes with my activity” 
	- Relatively modern (absent from Ancient world) 
	- Not incompatible with autocracy or with the absence of self-government 
	- Modern incarnation: Liberalism
