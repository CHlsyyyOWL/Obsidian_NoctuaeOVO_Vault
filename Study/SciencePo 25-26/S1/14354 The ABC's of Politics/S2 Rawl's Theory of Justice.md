## Veil of Ignorance

“In the original position, the parties are not allowed to know the social positions or the particular comprehensive doctrines of the persons they represent. They also do not know persons' race and ethnic group, sex, or various native endowments such as strength and intelligence, all within the normal range. We express these limits on infomation figuratively by saying the parties are behind a veil of ignorance.”

#### **What are the two key principles of justice?**

1. **Equal Basic Liberties Principle**
    - _Each person has the same indefeasible claim to a fully adequate scheme of equal basic liberties, which scheme is compatible with the same scheme of liberties for all._
    - Examples: freedom of thought, liberty of conscience, political rights (e.g., voting), freedom of association, rule of law, integrity of the person.
2. **Social and Economic Inequalities Principle**
    - Inequalities are permitted only if they meet **two conditions**:
        (a) they are attached to offices and positions open to all under **fair equality of opportunity**, and
        (b) they are to the **greatest benefit of the least-advantaged members of society** (_the difference principle_).
    
### **What is the difference principle?**

- The **difference principle** holds that social and economic inequalities (e.g., in income, wealth, authority) are justified **only if they improve the situation of the least-advantaged members of society** .
- It is not about equalizing everything, but about arranging inequalities so that those at the bottom are better off than they would be under any alternative scheme.

### **When are social or economic inequalities justified?**

- Inequalities are justified when:
    1. **Equal liberties are already secured** for everyone.
    2. **Opportunities are fair** (those with the same talents and effort should have the same prospects regardless of social class of birth).
    3. **The least advantaged benefit most** from the inequality (difference principle).
    
**What is the function of the original position?**
- The **original position** is a **hypothetical thought-experiment** to determine fair principles of justice .
- It represents a situation where free and equal persons decide the rules for society without knowing their personal circumstances.
- Its function: to **model fair conditions of agreement** that eliminate bargaining advantages rooted in luck, class, race, or historical contingency.
### **What is the veil of ignorance and how does it serve the original position?**
- The **veil of ignorance** is the condition that, in the original position, people do **not know**:
    - their class, race, gender, natural abilities, social position, wealth, or conception of the good life.
    
- This prevents bias and self-interest from distorting the choice of principles.
- It ensures that the chosen principles are **fair to all**, since no one can design rules to favor their own (unknown) position.

- **Two principles:** equal basic liberties + inequalities allowed only under fair opportunity and the difference principle.
- **Difference principle:** inequalities must benefit the least advantaged.
- **Inequalities justified:** only if liberties and fair opportunities are secured and the least advantaged gain.
- **Original position:** a fair starting point for choosing principles.
- **Veil of ignorance:** removes bias by hiding personal circumstances, ensuring impartiality.

# Seminar

**John Rawls's Goal**: To develop a conception if justice that any "free and rational person" would accept under fair condition 
- Original Position: A hypothetical scenario where individuals choose principles of justice without knowing their place in society 
- Self-Interest: The principle of justice are accepted because it serves all self-interests of individuals in the original position.
- Function of Principles: Once agreed upon, these principles guide:
	- All future agreements between individuals
	- Permissible forms of social cooperation
	- Types of governments and institutions that can be established (surprise)
=> Justice as Fairness
