Progressive Libertarianism:
- Defend the _negative liberties_ (freedom from state coercion), but also support certain _positive liberties_ like access to education, healthcare, and technology.
- Favor market mechanisms and decentralized solutions, but with an openness to regulation where market failures threaten dignity or equality (e.g., environmental protection, anti-discrimination law).

## Main Feature of Libertarianism

1. Private property 
	- Personal objects, productive capital and “public goods” 
	- Moral significance of private property => inviolable, absolute and foundational (self-owernship) 

2. Scepticism of authority 
	- Practical reasons: bureaucracy is not wise enough 
	- Moral reasons: no formal consent to Stat’s sovereignty

3. Free markets 
	- Related to self-ownership
		- The liberty to trade
		- The state should not intervene
	- No moral limit to exchange (sexual services, organs, drugs…)
	  
4. Market as spontaneous order 
	- Consensual rather than coerced 
	- Polycentric rather than direct 
	- Emergent rather than consciously designed
	  
5. Individualism 
	- Normative individualism => **Kantian principle (people as ends-in-themselves)** 
		- People should not be considered as **"Means"**
		- Taxes - People who get taxes, act like "means" for the greater good of society
	- Ethical individualism => Self-interest as a moral value (Rand)
		- You should consider your own interests

6. Negative liberty 
	- Maximalist conception of negative liberty (Spencer) 
		- Herber Spencer
		- The Law of Equal Freedom;
			- Freedom should be as large as possible
			- Free as long as nobody interferes 
	- Property-based conception
		- Unconstrained use of your own property

## Nozick and the political theory of libertarianism

Explain the emergence of State, with a starting point of state of Anarchy, through invisible hand. 

Robert Nozick (1938-2002) 
- publishes *Anarchy, State and Utopia* in 1974 
- Libertarianism Respond to John Rawls
	- Far from Liberal
	- Closer to Utilitarianism 
		- Difference Principle - Maximising social welfare 
	- Rawls Sacrifices liberty for equality 

Elements of context: 
- Rawlsian theory of justice => ASU = libertarian response to Rawls 
	- Libertarianism - Emphasises moral principle instead of the consequence
		- Lock - Individual property should be protected, people work (with nature state of property right)
		- Kantian - People ends in themselves
- Anarcho-individualist movement =>  State is immoral as such 
	- Anarcho-individualism starting point:
		- people do not have the freedom of consent to the state
		- recall - taxes
		- hence, state has no legitimacy 

Definition of the State: 
- Monopoly of violence ( de facto or de jure) 
- **Redistributive** dimension (taxes and universal protection) 

Conceptual distinction: 
- Minimal State (classical liberal State) 
	- the state is the entity that has to force its decisions
	- individuals who do not pay taxes enjoy same rights as those who did. 
- Ultraminimal State: State’s sovereignty restricted to those who formally contract with the State 
	- Illegitimate as (Anarcho-individualism)

Two main thesis: 
- Libertarian conception of the State (against anarcho-individualism) 
	- How can we defense the emerge of the state, in the state of Anarchy? 
- Libertarian conception of justice (against Rawls’ theory of justice)
	- distributive justice

### FROM THE STATE OF NATURE TO THE ULTRAMINIMAL STATE

Starting point = anarchy in the State of Nature 
- how can we explain the emergence of the State?
**Step1**: Different possibilities to solve conflicts 
- Self-defence 
- Protection Associations: 
	1) Mutual Protection 
	2) Professional associations 

**Step 2**: **concentration**
>Natural emergence of Monopolist (of power)

- Services provided by PA are rival => success of a PA relies on the failure of the others 
	- It is not a social contract, since it is not everyone considered.
- Invisible hand leads to the emergence of PA’ monopolies 
	- Private State: 
		- pay for company to defend yourself

At this stage, it is still not a State ! 
- PA don’t claim for **a monopoly of violence** 
- They don’t protect everybody
  
**Step 3**: Monopoly de facto (and de jure) 
- Market power of the **dominant agency** => judges conflicts between its members and between its members and private individuals (trust in its procedures)
- First element of the State =>**monopoly of violence (but only ultraminimal)** 

**Step 4**: ”Universal” protection (minimal State) 
- Need to compensate private individuals for the use of agency’s procedures 
- Therefore, offers protection for independents when they have a conflict with a member of the agency => form of “redistribution” (members pay for protection of non-members) 

>Question = morality of this “redistribution”? Nozick’s argument = **not a redistribution but a compensation**

> [!note]- Compensation and Redistribution 
> 1. 背景：从无政府到“最小国家”
> 
> 诺齐克面对无政府主义的挑战：如果每个人都可以自由选择保护机构（protective agencies），那么国家凭什么合法？
> 	•	在“无政府状态”下，不同的保护机构会彼此竞争甚至发生冲突。
> 	•	随着市场逻辑的发展，一个机构可能逐渐占据主导，成为“支配性保护机构”（dominant protective agency）。
> 	•	问题：这个支配性机构有权对所有人强制吗？如果有人没付钱，机构是否还应该保护他们？
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 2. 诺齐克的逻辑
> 
> 诺齐克认为：
> 	•	保护机构必须对“非客户”（没付钱的人）也提供基本保护。
> 	•	否则，假如机构只保护付费者，那么它在对付“非客户”时就会侵犯后者的自然权利（比如把他们当作“合法攻击对象”）。
> 	•	因此，支配性机构不得不为所有人提供最低限度的安全保障。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 3. 为什么是“补偿”而不是“再分配”？
> 
> 这里的措辞非常重要：
> 	•	如果国家对未付费者提供保护，看起来像是“给了他们好处”，会让人以为是再分配。
> 	•	但诺齐克强调，这不是为了平等或福利，而是为了避免对未付费者侵犯权利。
> 	•	因为支配性机构在事实上阻止了其他小型保护机构的运行，它剥夺了非客户的选择权。
> 	•	所以，对未付费者的保护是对他们被“排除选择”的一种补偿（compensation），而不是为了社会正义而强加的再分配。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 4. 关键区分
> 	•	再分配（redistribution）：国家为了“模式化的平等”把A的钱转给B。
> 	•	补偿（compensation）：当A的行为限制了B的自然自由或选择时，A必须给B一个“防止受害”的保障。
> 
> 诺齐克要避免掉进“福利国家”的逻辑，所以他必须坚持：国家的普遍保护是出于补偿责任，而不是追求结果平等。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> ✅ 总结：
> 在诺齐克那里，支配性保护机构必须保护没付费的人，不是因为要搞福利或平等，而是因为它通过垄断限制了他们的自由选择。对这些人提供保护，是“补偿他们被排除的机会”，而不是“再分配资源”。这样，国家就能在不违反个人权利的前提下，从无政府走向最小国家。

### Critique of Rawlsian Theory of Justice

**Three main problems with Rawlsian conception:** 

- Consequentialist approach => problem are not consequences but principles 
	- should not focus the consequence of justice, but the principle of justice
	- negative consequences does not matter, as long as there is **good and just principle**.

- No good explanation to justify social cooperation 
	- Rawl cannot explaine why would the person of greatest advantage would have the interests of social cooperation
	- Social cooperation is because there have to be compensation to those less advantaged.

- Continuous intervention of the State: 
	“no distributional patterned principle of justice can be continuously realized with continuous interference with people’s lives […] To maintain a pattern one must either continually interfere to stop people from transferring resources as they wish to, or continually interfere to take from some persons resources that others for some reason chose to transfer to them” 

### AN ALTERNATIVE CONCEPTION OF JUSTICE 

- Nozick’s theory  based on three principles: 
	- Justice of acquisition:
		- Locke's theory of acquisition
		- “A person who acquires a holding in accordance with the principle of justice in acquisition is entitled to that holding” 
		- Does not worsen the situation of others
			- Cannot hold the only water hole in the community
	- Justice of transfer
		- “A person who acquires a holding in accordance with the principle of justice in transfer, from someone else entitled to the holding, is entitled to the holding” 
	- “No one is entitled to a holding except by (repeated) applications of 1 and 2” 
- Decision difference: 
	- For Rawls, distribution is just if it fits the structure of just distribution => structural conception of justice 
	- For Nozick, distribution is just if it arises from another distribution by legitimate means => historical conception of justice
