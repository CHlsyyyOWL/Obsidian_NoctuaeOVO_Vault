## Absolute Contractualism - Hobbes

**- Thomas Hobbes (1588 – 1679) 
- Founding father of political philosophy 
- Leviathan published 
- Political context:  Civil War in England in the mid-17th century / Aftermath of Wars of Religion in Europe 
- Scientific background: application of science to political problems 
- Method: divide scientific problems into different parts

**Background**
- Civil war
- Natural scientists
- trend of political philosophy:
	- philosophers think abstract, discussing "the good", "moral"...instead of trying to solve real problems
- Hence, Political Science: 
	- Following a scientific research way, breaking down real political problem into small fractional questions
	- Studying political problems scientificly, going through each small elements of society to analyze it
	- The smallest element - individuals

### Leviathan and the Escape from the State of Nature
- State of nature = product of Hobbes’ scientific method -> heuristic tool (not an anthropology nor a historical event) 
	- State of nature, is a methodological method, it does not exists by itself, but being constructed to solve problem
	- State of nature, is not a anthropology: it does not mean people are bad in nature, but people preferred to behave in a way, 
- Main characteristics: 
	- People are rational and follow their interests 
	- Men are equal -> war of all against all = product of equality
		- No men, is able to dominate others. 
		- Poverty does not exists, but being created by one stealing the others. 
- Hence, **state of nature is a state of war.
	- Living in a state of uncertainty
	- In the absence of a state, people have no trust of each other. 
		- people have unlimited freedom to follow their own interests to behave
		- people are immanent to the concentration of power. 
	- State of War is not the product of human nature, but a product of the absence of the state. 
- Thus, how do we overcome the state of nature? - **Construction of sovereign contract**
	- through the contract, people gave up their actual freedom, in exchange of their social freedom 
	- This is called Leviathan.

> "Nature hath made men so equal, in the faculties of the body, and mind; as that though there be found one man sometimes manifestly stronger in body, or of quicker mind than another; yet when all is reckoned together, the difference between man, and man, is not so considerable, as that one man can thereupon claim to himself any benefit, to which another may not pretend, as well as he. For as to the strength of body, the weakest has strength enough to kill the strongest, either by secret machination, or by confederacy with others, that are in the same danger with himself.“

- Solution to the State of War = sovereign contract (renouncement to absolute freedom) 
- Absolute conception of the contract -> sovereign is not bound by the contract
  
## The Liberal Contract - John Locke

- Locke (1632 – 1704) 
- Greater influence than Hobbes on political modernity and political institution -> spiritual father of the Constitution of the United States 
- Different starting part: restrictions to the absolute power of the sovereign
	- Not only to found the sovereign power, but to limit the sovereign power.

**Nature Rights
- State of Nature = Genesis (subsistence through the appropriation of nature)
- Natural rights = pre-political -> religious origin  
	- Human being has pre-political rights, existed before the state. 
- Three natural rights: 
	- Life 
	- Liberty 
	- Property
		- Property rights express the domination of Men over Nature:
			- Mankind has to work and against over the nature, in order to survive. 
			- Hence, the existence of poverty, is because people has to possess the nature (property). 
		  
			>“§. 26. God, who has given the world to men in common, has also given them reason to make use of it to the best advantage of life, and convenience. The earth, and all that is therein, is given to men for the support and comfort of their being. And though all the fruits it naturally produces, and beasts it feeds, belong to mankind in common, as they are produced by the spontaneous hand of nature; and nobody has originally a private dominion, exclusive of the rest of mankind, in any of them, as they are thus in their natural state: yet being given for the use of men, there must of necessity be a means to appropriate them some way or other, before they can be of any use, or at all beneficial to any particular man. The fruit, or venison, which nourishes the wild Indian, who knows no enclosure, and is still a tenant in common, must be his, and so his, i.e. a part of him, that another can no longer have any right to it, before it can do him any good for the support of his life.”
			
		- Self-ownernship -> people possess the fruits of their labour 
			- critique of slavery, since slavery is against the idea of self-ownership
			- You are only able to process what you are working on 
		  
			>"§. 27. Though the earth, and all inferior creatures, be common to all men, yet every man has a property in his own person: this nobody has any right to but himself. The labor of his body, and the work of his hands, we may say are properly his. Whatsoever then he removes out of the state that nature has provided, and left it in, he has mixed his labor with, and joined to it something that is his own, and thereby makes it his property. It being by him removed from the common state nature has placed it in, it has by this labor something annexed to it, that excludes the common right of other men: for this labor being the unquestionable property of the laborer, no man but he can have a right to what that is once joined to, at least where there is enough, and as good, left in common for others."
			
		- Possibility of fair exchange -> exchange = based on consent 
			- Nature, achieved by a form of exchange of social nature

## Contemporary Liberlism: John Rawls

- Rawls (1921-2002)  = Revival of political theory and philosophy 
	- Intellectual context: hegemony of positivist social sciences 
	- Political context: civil rights movement, demands for justice
	    
- Guiding questions: 
	- Are there universal values of justice? If yes, what are they? 
	- How can PT contribute to improve reality? 
		
- Rawlsian approach

 Rawls:
 - Making a theory of Justice (Just Society, instead of "good" society)
- Believe his theory is to improve politics, might not directly involve
- Focus on the central debate of Liberality - Equality

### Rawlsian Veil of Ignorance

#### The need for social cooperation

Two Difficulties:
- impossibility to refer to pre-political values 
- No need to revise the state of nature

Main premise: society is based on social cooperation
- Social cooperation 
	- Is guided by ”publicly recognized rules and procedures which those cooperating accept as appropriate to regulate their conduct” 
	- Includes the idea of “fair terms” (reciprocity and mutuality) 
	- Each participant must have a rational advantage in cooperation 
	  
- Rules of justice = moral framework for cooperation

#### The Original Position

Question: How to establish universal values of justice? 
Answer: revival of the “State of Nature” (hypothetical not historical)

- Rational people 
- Veil of ignorance 

“In the original position, the parties are not allowed to know the social positions […] of the persons they represent. They also do not know person’s race and ethnic group, sex or various native endowments such as strength and intelligence, all within the normal range”

Therefore => principles of justice shall not result from your actual social, racial, gender position

#### Principle of Justice

Assumptions about OP:
- People are **rational individuals** = guided by their **interests** (methodological individualism) 
- People are “**risk-avoider**” (maximin): people try to maximise their welfare in the worst-case scenario 

Two principles of justice: 
- 1- Each person has the same indefeasible claim to a fully adequate scheme equal basic liberties, which scheme is compatible with the same scheme of liberties for all 
- 2- Social and economic inequalities are to satisfy two conditions: 
	- They are to be attached to offices and positions open to all under conditions of fair **equality of opportunity**
	- Second, they are to be to the greatest benefit of the least-advantaged members of society (the difference principle)

> [!note]- Self-Interests
> 1. Rawls 的设定
> 
> 在 “无知之幕”（veil of ignorance） 下，所有做选择的人都不知道自己在社会中的具体地位：
> 	•	不知道自己会是富人还是穷人；
> 	•	不知道自己是多数还是少数；
> 	•	不知道自己的天赋、性格、性别、种族等等。
> 
> 因此，理论上他们必须以一种“普遍的理性”来设计制度，而不是带着既得利益的视角。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 2. 你的问题：是不是还是自利？
> 
> 确实，Rawls 承认，在无知之幕中人们依然是理性的、自利的（self-interested rational agents）。只是，这个自利是“抽象的、去个体化的自利”。
> 
> 换句话说：
> 	•	他们不是说“我现在是富人，所以我要保护富人的利益”；
> 	•	而是说“假如我将来可能是任何人，那么我应该设计一个制度，使得无论我落在社会哪一层次，都能接受”。
> 
> 因此，他们会选择一个风险最小化的制度。Rawls 认为这会导向他的 差别原则（difference principle）：
> 
> 社会经济不平等只有在对最弱势群体有利时才是正当的。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 3. 为什么不是功利主义？
> 
> 你提出的担忧很像对 Rawls 的经典批评：
> 	•	如果人们只是“计算自己可能在哪个位置”，那他们可能采用期望效用最大化，从而回到功利主义（maximize expected utility）。
> 	•	Rawls 反驳说，在无知之幕后，因为没有任何概率分布（你不能知道自己成为穷人的概率是 10% 还是 90%），所以人们会采取一种极小化风险的策略（maximin rule）：选择最坏情况下最好的结果。
> 
> 这也是他为什么认为人们不会冒险去选择功利主义（因为那可能导致某些人被牺牲在“最大幸福”之下）。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> 4. 学界的批评
> 	•	哈特（H.L.A. Hart）和哈耶克都批评 Rawls：他设想的人其实还是在计算自利，只是换了一种方式，为什么一定会选择 maximin 而不是 expected utility？
> 	•	**阿马蒂亚·森（Amartya Sen）**则进一步指出，正义不仅是制度设计的问题，还涉及人们现实中的能力和机会。
> 
> ⸻
> 
> ✅ 总结：
> 是的，在无知之幕后的人仍然是自利的，但他们的自利是不确定身份下的自利。正因为他们不知道自己将落在社会的哪个位置，他们被迫以一种“普遍可接受”的方式来设想制度，这就是 Rawls 的理论创新。
>
