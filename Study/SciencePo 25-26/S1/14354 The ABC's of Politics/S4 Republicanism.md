# The Main Features of Republicanism

Predominance of the community 
- Ontological / Anthropological => community comes first (social dimension of human beings) 
	- Human are naturally social beings
- Normative => social relations are self-fulfilling 
	- We need to have social connections to be fully human

Social contract is mainly horizontal 
- State is not a third-party but rather the mean of realisation of the political community
- Contract
	- exists among citizens
	- is social by nature, moral and ethical.

Individuals are citizens - not mere individuals 
- Formal: people have individual rights 
	- citizenship
	- a set of rights 
- Normative: *active* participation is a duty and should seek the Common good
	- virtues engagement 
	- for the common good 
	- active in the public sphere with a set of duties

Defence of substantial conception of the good 
- Conception of the "good"
	- The conception of "good" in liberalism is private ( can only exists with relation to other individuals)
	- Something **collective**, can only be in a political community (Hannah Arendt)
	- have **general interests** - compared to only "individual interest"
- Ethical conception of politics

# The Origin of Republicanism

## Ancient origin 

Aristotle = democratic ideal of Ancient Greece 
- Human beings as zoon politikon 
- Ethical conception of the political 
	- A good of politics is to achieve the common good life
- Distinction between public and private spheres
	- a explicitly of public sphere compared to private sphere
	- Public sphere
		- equality between citizens
		- You do not follow own interest, but act in common
	- Private sphere
		- allowing for dominance (slave)
		- satisfying self-interests, biological needs

Roman Republic (Cicero) 
- The political must seek common interest of the people 
	- Cannot have a republican having democratic model
	- Republican try to act in the common good of people
- Power = magistrates and Senate 
- Mix constitution

## Italian Revival

Italian Renaissance and the revival of the vita activa 
- Palmieri (1406-1475) • Salutatti (1331-1406) 
- Leonardo Bruni (1370-1444)

Decisive contribution of **Macchiavelli** 
- Citizens are naturally "good", instead of they "should be good".
- Defence of the superiority of the Republic 
- Conflictual republicanism 
	- positive role of antagonism 
		- introduces dynamic to public sphere, which could lead to greater good 
		- how can political institution can transfer conflict into postive outcome
		- how can political institution open to public
- Central role of the *plebs* (least advantage person in society)
	- the people introduce dynamic to the public (if open to conflict)

## Modern republican contractualism  - Rousseau

Jean-Jacques Rousseau 
- State of problem: **transition from natural to social freedom(collective freedom)** 
	- Not all against all, but social cooperation
	- preserve freedom, reach higher freedom - the aim for social contract 

### "On the social contract"

“To find a form of association which will **defend and protect with the whole common force the person and goods of each associate**, and in which each, while uniting with all, **may still obey only himself, and remain as free as before**.” 

- Political institution: instead of violation of freedom, but a condition for freedom to exists. 

**Duality** of the social contract: alienation of *individual freedom vs higher stage of freedom*: 

“Each of us puts in common his person and all his power under the supreme direction of the **general will**; and we receive each member as an indivisible part of the whole” 

Concept of popular sovereignty: the coexistence of individual and collective sovereignty, instead of individual sovereignty on its own.
- Inalienable 
	- Hobbes: people alienate sovereignty to leviathan
	- Rousseau: always can re-claim sovereignty
- Indivisible 
	- cannot be break up into different powers
	- sovereignty is **General**
- **General will** (volonté générale)
	- <font color="#c0504d"> is not the aggregation of individual interest</font>
	- is expression of general good, in representation of every individual. 
	- that is to say, behave as a"citizen", seeks for **the common good**

How can we reach general will? - voting ？

## Modern version of republicanism - Hannah Arendt

Arendt (1906-1975) 

Conceptual distinction between vita *contemplativa* (philosophy) and vita *activa* (three components) 

Vita Contemplativa:
- search for internal truth and essence
- should not get involved into human actions (only for metaphysical truth)

Vita Activa
- Labor
	- to fulfill biological needs 
	- animal laborant
- Work: production of objects 
	- homo faber 
	- projects are already part of the world
	- produce to transdecent 
- Action: political action
	- realisation of human being through belonging to a social and political action
	- zoon politikon

Two main problems: 
- Divorce between philosophy and politics 
	- back to Plato
- Reduction of the political to the social activities
	- no longer concern with political concern but with social activities 
	- go back to a ancient concept of politics 
		- real "politics" can be found in the work of Aristotle
