# Notion of social contract -  legitimacy of power 

Two types of question: 
    Conceptual question: what is power?
    Analytical question: how does power work? 
    
Distinction between power and domination / authority (coercion vs consent) 
    Structuralist vs relational approach 
    Descriptive vs critical approach 

## I. General definitions 

### What is power?
- Power as an actuality or a potentiality 
- Power as individual or collective 
- Power as active or passive 
- Power as measure -> scope vs outcomes of power 
- Power as a general capacity (potential) or as power over people (potestas) 

![[S6 Power - Foucult.png]]
### The three dimensional conception of power (Lukes)

- One-dimensional: “A has the power over B to the extent that he can get B to do something that B would not otherwise do” (coercion)

- Two-dimensional: “Power is also exercised when A devotes his energies to creating or reinforcing social and political values and institutional practices that limit the scope of the political process to public consideration of only those issues which comparatively innocuous to A”
  
- Three-dimensional : “the capacity to shape interests against people’s objective interests”>)

### From power to domination

Radical approach to power = beyond mere coercion (unwilling compliance) 

What’s wrong with domination? 
- “Just” forms of domination? Paternalistic power, transformative power… 
- Underlying idea = domination is a form of power with a false/wrong compliance 

![[S6 Power - Foucult-1.png]]

## II. Main approaches of power and domination 

Concept of domination = mainly normative, critical and political 
    
Domination = usually connected with a meta-theory 🡪 discrepancy between objective and subjective interests

### I. Descriptive approach (Weber) 
![[S6 Power - Foucult-2.png]]

### II. Structuralist approach (Marx, Bourdieu) 

First assumption: domination = stable (domination as product of a social structure) 
Second assumption: critical approach to domination (needs to be unveiled) 

Example: the question of Taylorism 
- Weberian explanation: product of modern rationality 
- Marxian explanation: discipline of the workers (real subsumption of the labour-process); “efficiency” = expression of the interests of the ruling class

#### Louis Althusser
Wrong understanding of power 
- repressive rather than productive 
- power reduced to repressive State Apparatus 

- First thesis: “ideology represents individual’s imaginary relations to their real conditions of existence” 
- Second thesis: “Ideology has a material existence” 
- Third thesis : “Ideology is based on interpellation” 

#### Bourdieu's Approach
Between Marx (ideology), Weber (legitimation process), Elias (cultural and symbolic dimensions) 

First assumption: domination is less political or economic than cultural (symbolic violence) 

Second assumption: domination is incorporated (habitus) 

### III. Relational approach (Foucault)

The main targets of Foucault: 
- Marxist conception of power 
	- First problem -> power is too “substantial” (power is the property of actors) 
	- Second problem -> power is reduced to economic sphere

Sovereign approach to power -> common to Law and Political Theory ; power as a legal question 
	- Outdated conception: relies on the problem of power as a problem of sovereignty 
	- Modern societies = plurality of the sources of power 

#### Elements of Power
1. relational approach to power 
2. relationship between power and knowledge 
3. body as the object of power 
4. microphysics of power
