# Reading

该段文字主要论述了在当前气候变化危机下，“绿色资本主义”及其关键政策工具“绿色凯恩斯主义”的局限性与内在矛盾。作者指出，全球资本主义的扩张积累是气候变化等环境问题的根本原因，而将气候变化简单地视为“市场失灵”并试图通过市场机制（如碳税、碳市场）来解决是徒劳的。绿色凯恩斯主义主张通过政府主导的财政刺激和监管来推动绿色转型，但在全球金融化、民族国家主权限制以及资本逐利本质的制约下，这种方案难以有效实现。最终，文本认为，解决气候危机需要的全球性管理似乎指向了行星主权的出现，即一个能够协调投资、抑制自由搭便车的**“气候利维坦”**

# Script

### 1
Based on the market logic explained by Carmen, therefore the Green Keynesian emerged after 2008 as a response to the 2008 financial crisis. Financial Institutions no longer believe in the invisible hand and the state took its responsibility. 

Green Keynesian is a set of fiscal activist policies which seek to stabilise capitalism and address climate change by returning the state to a central in managing investment, employment, and economic demand with an explicit environment orientation. 

It was much triggered by market failure and was called the Green Sweet Spot for the economy, and focusing on fiscal policies since it requires sector-specific policies while monetary policies only focus on the general price level. 

## 2
The Green Keynesian was prevailing at the moment while its effectiveness was limited by the global economics and politics.

Among the free global investment market which depends much on speculative demand and trust investment, anything signalling Green Keynesian might not work could cause severe loses. Politically, the Green Keynesian requires a vigorous state, however it is indeterminate to a state's ability to deliver ecological transformation.

 With the fact that states are loosing its sovereignty in both finance and politics, as state no longer possess fiscal-monetary autonomy due to free exchange rate and capital flow, the Green Keynesian actually failed to image the work required might be done without the state and therefore cannot conceive a world where there are multiple social fields and require organisational work. 

## 3

Do we see examples or clues for Climate Leviathan in real life? In the end of Section4, the authors mentioned China and comment that China make Climate Leviathan more possible than Behemoth at present stage. They commented that China are more likely to advocate capitalist planetary management than accept global disorder, and their elites recognise their self-interests requires global carbon mitigation and adaptation to rapid climate change. 

However, as we said, it is only for present stage and certainly they mentioned many challenges. Since China lack parliamentary - democratic state and multiparty elections, capitalists state in China are exceptional and any signals of dissent would be suppressed. At some point this century, the local, regional, and planetary effects of China-centered global  commodity production will coalesce, accentuating the social and ecological contradictions of the current order. How will the Chinese state respond? They asks,an abstract ideological commitment to a harmonious society advocated by the Communist Party of China, in no way guarantees its realisation. **Any attempt to build a “harmonious”“green” capitalist future will force the question of planetary sovereignty.**


## 4
And therefore we have reached our final part of the argument. From Green Keynesian to Climate Leviathan, Capitalism needs a planetary manager, a Keynesian world state. But elites were reluctant to build it. and a Transnational Keynesianism can only be predicated on the consolidation of a transnational variation on the sovereign subject without which Keynesianism is inconceivable. 

Facing this issue, the authors proposed two possible solutions that will both fill the roll for Leviathan: First is constructing a consensual global agreement that will at least reach something better than status quo，which will still face the problem of collective action and is unrealisable ; then we have the second option, which is the emergence of one nation-state, or a small set of nation states that arrogate to themselves the impossible  institutional capacities that come with an interest in supranational “ecological stimulus. **And that, is Climate Leviathan**,that can bear the burdens required of a planetary Keynesian subject, capable of  coordinating investment, distributing productive and destructive capacity, and managing free riders.
