# Aims of this Class

- Revisit Sociology as an empirical social science.
	- Learn more new topics and theoretical approaches
	- Discuss several empirical strategies and research designs.
	- Carrying on your own research

- Discuss in-depth one sociological empirical investigation.
	- theoretical
	- methodological 
	- Epistemological
	- Ethical
	- Practical 

- Provide tools to understand and to be critical of empirical social research, and to design empirical investigations. 

# History of Empirical Research in the Social Sciences

- Before the I WW 
	- Few sociologists carry out empirical work. Ex. Émile Durkheim
	- Quantitative social surveys are fielded, mostly in Europe, to analyze poverty and living conditions. These surveys have shortcomings. 
		- Statistical measurements are not well-developed at that moment; both depend on methodological development and physical ability, such as the use of a PC. 

- Interwar Period:
	- Empirical research has developed more in the US. (Chicago school, Columbia)
	- Case Studies vs. Statistical Methods 
		- Raisons Pratiques, 1994; Le sens Pratique, 1980 - by 布尔迪厄（Pierre Bourdieu） refers to "The privilege of Practice" that field research is to a great extent explanatory, thus biased. 
		- 克利福德·格尔茨（Clifford Geertz) In《文化的解释》（The Interpretation of Cultures, 1973）中， emphasizes that "thick description" is actually explanatory compared to objective. 
	- Advances in Statistical Methods

- After WWII

- 1970 - 1990: the paradigm wars 
	- Postivism versus hermeneutics / phenomenology
	- Debates on the epistemological assumptions behind methods

- 1990 Onwards
	- Increasing methodological specialization
	- Increasingly mixed-methods
	- "The dictatorship of the research question"
