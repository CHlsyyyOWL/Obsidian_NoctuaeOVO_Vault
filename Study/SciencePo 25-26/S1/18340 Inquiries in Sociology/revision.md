# Notes
## Epistemology 认识论
- (Neo-)positivism 新实证主义
	- 社会事实客观存在
	- 可被测量、比较
	- 目标：解释、因果、一般化
- Interpretivism / Humanism 解释性人文主义
	- 社会现实是建构的
	- 意义来自行动者理解
	- 目标：理解、情境化、意义

## Methodology
### Ethnography 民族志
- **Desmond, Evicted**
- **Goffman, On the Run**

**Definition：**
- Ethnography is a qualitative method based on long-term immersion in a social setting, aiming to understand social practices and meanings from the perspective of participants.


**What it answers**
- How social life is experienced
- Processes, not just outcomes
- Meanings, norms, informal rules

**Strengths**
- Captures context and meaning
	人的行为**嵌入在结构与关系中**
	你看不到 context，就会误判行为
	Desmond 的例子：
	- “看起来不理性”的租客行为
	    → 在 eviction 压力下是**理性的生存策略**
- Suitable for hidden or poorly understood groups
	- Hidden population
	- 警察追捕对象
	- 非正规工作者
	- 贫困群体
- Reveals mechanisms invisible to surveys

**Limitations**
- Limited generalizability
	- but analytically insightful 
- Time-consuming
- Researcher bias
- Ethical risks (distance, involvement)
  
**When to use**
- When little is known about a group
- When insider perspectives matter
- When processes matter more than outcomes

**Ethical issues**
- **Informed consent** in long-term fieldwork
	- In ethnographic research, consent is often informal and ongoing rather than formally obtained at one point in time, which raises ethical concerns regarding participants’ understanding and autonomy.
- Blurred boundaries between researcher and participants
- Engaging in or witnessing illegal activities

### Interview 访谈
- Interviews with graduates
- Interviews in _Crime in the Making_ (life histories

**Definition：**
Interviews are qualitative methods based on direct interaction between researcher and respondent to collect narratives, perceptions, and experiences.
**What it answers**
- Individual experiences
- Perceptions, motivations, interpretations
**Strengths**
- Flexibility
- Depth of understanding
- Suitable for sensitive topics

**Limitations**
- **Social desirability bias**
	- Social desirability bias occurs when respondents adjust their answers to appear socially acceptable or favorable to the interviewer.
- Memory bias
- Limited representativeness
  
**When to use**
- When subjective experience matters
- When you want depth but not full immersion

**Ethical issues**
 - Confidentiality
- Emotional burden on participants
### Focus Group 焦点小组
- **Children research – L’enfance de l’ordre**

**Definition：**
Focus groups are group interviews where interaction between participants is used to generate data.

**What it answers**
- How opinions are formed
- **Norms**, hierarchies, group dynamics

**Strengths**
- Interaction produces richer discussion
- Cost-effective
- Useful for exploratory research

**Limitations**
- Dominant participants
- Conformity pressure
- Limited depth on personal experiences
  
**When to use**
- When collective norms matter
- When participants share a common experience

**Ethical issues**
- Confidentiality harder to guarantee
- Power relations within the group
### Survey 调查研究
- **World Values Survey** 
- Graduate employment surveys

**Definition：**
Surveys collect standardized data from a large number of individuals using questionnaires.

**What it answers**
- Distribution of characteristics
- Correlations and comparisons
- Trends across groups

**Strengths**
- **Representativeness**
- Comparability
- Generalizable results

**Limitations**
- Limited depth
- Self-report bias
- Cannot establish causality  

**When to use**
- When you want patterns
- When comparison matters

**Ethical issues**
- Data protection
- Informed consent
### Experiment 社会实验
- **Pedulla (2016)** labor market discrimination

**Definition：**
Experiments manipulate an independent variable to observe its causal effect on an outcome, using control and **randomization.**

**What it answers**
- identify and establish Causal relationships

**Strengths**
- **Strong internal validity**
- Clear causal inference

**Limitations**
- Artificial settings
- **Limited external validity**
- Ethical constraints
  
**When to use**
- **When causality is central**
- When manipulation is possible

**Ethical issues**
- Deception
- Harm to participants
- Informed consent
### Longitudinal 纵向研究（长期性研究）
**Definition：**
Studies following the same individuals over time to analyze trajectories and change.
**What it answers**
Longitudinal studies answer questions about change, trajectories, and timing.**
**Strengths**
- Captures dynamics and turning points
**Limitations**
- Attrition
- Costly and time-consuming  
**When to use**
 Longitudinal studies are particularly suited to analysing life-course dynamics and identifying turning points in social trajectories.

**Ethical issues**
informal consent

### Mixed Methods 混合方法
Combining quantitative and qualitative methods in one research design.
