# What is an Inquiry in Sociology ?

| Sociology Inquiry                           |
| ------------------------------------------- |
| Sociology dont judge                        |
| Patterns, methods                           |
| Dealing with social facts                   |
| We dont always know what we are looking for |
- Non-Falsifiable 
- Reasoning, theory and empirical proof - analogical reasoning
- Interpretative, Comprehensive 

| Aspect                 | Positivism                                                          | Interpretative/Comprehensive |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| View of Social Reality | Social world is objective                                           |                              |
| Goal of Research       | Discover universal Laws or patterns, Causality like "why x cause y" |                              |
| Role of Researcher     | Detached Observer: Neutral, objective, invisible                    |                              |
| Ket Question           | What are the social facts and their causes                          |                              |
- Select one you are curious of it badly
- start from a question that you would make a sense to the public 
- better if echo with your own life experience 
- think of a group and organization that you are gonna as a "world of mutual relations" that you would dive into
- try to think something you might think "strange" that seems it distant from your world (de-naturalize those situations, if your are able to question your own perceptions)
- Privilege a topic, a group or a fortiori an organization that is permanent enough, that leaves you sufficient marks to study (written posts, documents, archives)  

- Practical (feasible)
- Giving rising to some initial questions
- Associated to one community, place, or organization to investigate
- Linked to socio-historical context that you can understand
- Adapted to qualitative methods (interviews, observations)
