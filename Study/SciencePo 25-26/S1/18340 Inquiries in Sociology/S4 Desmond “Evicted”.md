# Reading: Matthew Desmond, Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City
## **📌 核心论点**

- 驱逐不仅是贫困的结果，更是**再生产贫困的机制**。
- 低收入租房家庭：高住房负担 + 正式与非正式驱逐 → 居住不稳定、健康受损、社会资本崩塌 → 城市不平等加深。
- 密尔沃基案例：2009–2011 年约 **1/8 租户**两年内至少经历一次被迫迁居；其中 **近半为非正式驱逐**
## **🛠 研究设计与方法**

- **田野调查**：2008–2009 年，南北区社区 + 拖车公园；深描 8 个家庭、房东、法院与市政互动。
- **多方法整合**：
    - 民族志（relational ethnography）
    - Milwaukee Area Renters Study (MARS)
    - 法院档案 (2003–2013)
    - 驱逐庭现场观察
    
- **方法论**：把住房市场—地方执法—福利—房东经营看作互嵌关系。
    
## **🔗 机制链条**

1. **住房成本挤压**：>50% 收入用于住房，1/4 家庭住房负担率 >70%。
2. **断供与行政执法**：公用事业断供 + 违规执法 → 驱逐触发；季节性效应明显。
3. **正式 +非正式双轨**：法院判决 + 私下驱赶（卸门、买断、威胁）。
4. **负面外溢**：驱逐 → 居住质量下降、就业流失、健康恶化。
5. **选择性脆弱**：有孩家庭被驱逐概率约为无孩家庭的三倍。

## **📊 关键事实**

- **10.8%**：近一年内搬家的租户，其最近一次搬家属于“被迫迁居”。
- **3%**：若只计正式驱逐，比例骤降。
- **16 户/日**：密尔沃基法院系统平均每日驱逐家庭数。
    
## **🏛 政策含义**

- **住房政策转向**：普惠化住房券（替代所有权偏置的补贴）。
- **司法改革**：法律援助、限制驱逐记录长期污名化。
- **行政改良**：改进断供与违章处理，减少“执法—驱逐”的自动耦合。
    

## **🔍 与制度政治经济学的对接**

- **国家能力负向测试**：驱逐可视为地方政府治理不平等的体现。
- **抵押品渠道**：住房成本和信用受损削弱家庭资产流动性，可嵌入你的 PIH-LTV 模型。
- **性别/家庭效应**：儿童存在 → 驱逐概率大幅提高。
- **形式/非形式双轨**：与威权治理中的“灰色合约”可做比较。
    
## **📝 可操作研究题**

- **劳动供给效应**：驱逐冲击与短期就业。
- **季节性制度耦合**：断供禁令起止与驱逐高峰。
- **儿童与司法裁决**：复刻密尔沃基法院微观回归，比较其他州异质性。

# Lecture

## The Ethnographic Method

- Long-term immersion in a particular setting, with the aim to describe and understand a social group, institution or situation.
- Epistemological assumptions and grounded theory
	- a way of generating knowledge

- Three main features:
	- Unfamiliarity
		- you have to be unfamiliar with your observer
	- Context
		- the context should be important so worthy for ethnographic study
	- Induction
		- if wan to be theory focused
			- instead of "knowing a theory in advance, and testing the theory"
			- generate a theory from the context

- External or participant observation
	- External
		- explicitly from the situation, recording by camera etc. Not getting the feeling of being part of it. Ex. You cannot be a participant of kids...
		- Do not want to affect the situation, influence how people behave
	- Participant
		- be inside the situation (immerse), experience the same thing 

### The First Ethnographers

B. Mainowski, an anthropologist, spends time in "exile" in the Trobriand Islands, observing, and produces the first enthographic study.
- His work criticises the "evolutionary" approach that was dominant in anthropology at that time
- Functionalist perspective

1960s-1970s, becomes a very popular method of study, in the idea of to document social life from a "native perspective", using many sources
- C. Geertz "Thich description"

Problems
- Lack of Theory
- Accusations of manipulating evidence
	- not being objective enough 

### Implementation

 When should this method being used?
 - We dont know much about the group/institution
 - There might be differences in perception between outsiders and insiders
 - The group or phenomenon is somewhat hidden for mainstream society

Difficulties:
- Access to fieldwork, being accepted by the group (what to reveal or not)
- conflict between participating and taking an external approach
- Practical difficulties (recording information, taking notes)
- Ethical Issues
- Leaving the Field

## Evicted: Poverty and Profit in America City (2016)

- How housing plays a roll in understanding poverty [[Explore Econ 2025 Presentation Prep]] #economics/housing

### Main Approach and Context
- Object of the research: Eviction as a process
- Milwaukee, 2008-2010
- Empirical Strategy
	- First observation of a trailer park that was gping to be evicted (Larrine)
	- Following families experiencing eviction (Arleen)
	- Following a landlady (Sherrena)
### More on the Empirical Strategy
- Access to the fieldwork: **Woo** (Ket informant)
- The researcher introduces himself as a **writer**
	- Records conversations, takes extensive notes
	- Do fact-checking 
		- Someone say they are evicted because of some reason, he go and check whether if it is the case
- How to negotiate a privilege position 
- Dilemmas: help-intervening or not

### General Results
- High eviction rates are a result of the market (supply, demand, price setting). Prices are over tge accessibility threshold in poor neighberhoods (rent prices vary less than average wages by neighbourhood).
	- This lead families to forgo paying rent
- Eviction processes are bureaucratised and can be very quick. It can be part of business model.
- Black women with children are the most frequent profile of evicted family.
- Residential stability is crucial for psychological, educational, employment, social and community stability.
