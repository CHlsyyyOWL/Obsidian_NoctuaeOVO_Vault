### Slide A: IMF Package — Policy and Results

**Slide上写的内容（英文，bullet points）：**

- Thailand, Indonesia, South Korea received emergency packages totaling ~USD 110 billion
- Conditions: fiscal austerity, high interest rates, structural reform, closure of insolvent financial institutions
- South Korea: USD 58.4bn package, foreign ownership ceiling raised 26% → 100%, 787 financial institutions closed/merged
- Short-term cost: GDP contraction (Korea approx. -5.7% in 1998), rising unemployment
- Recovery: Korea's GDP growth returned to +10.7% by 1999

**中文讲解：**

三个国家接受了IMF援助，总额大约1100亿美元。IMF的条件是标准的"华盛顿共识"药方：财政紧缩、加息保卫货币、开放外资准入、关闭资不抵债的金融机构。韩国是最典型的案例，拿了584亿美元，代价是外资持股上限从26%直接提到100%，787家金融机构被关停或合并。短期代价非常大，1998年GDP负增长5.7%左右，失业率翻倍。但恢复也很快，1999年GDP增速就反弹到10.7%。

**Speaker Note：**

Three countries accepted IMF emergency assistance totaling approximately 110 billion dollars. The conditions followed the standard IMF playbook: fiscal austerity, high interest rates to defend the currency, structural reforms opening markets to foreign ownership, and closure of insolvent financial institutions. South Korea is the most illustrative case. It received 58.4 billion dollars. In return, the foreign ownership ceiling was raised from 26% to 100%, and 787 financial institutions were closed or merged. The short-term cost was severe: GDP contracted by approximately 5.7% in 1998 and unemployment doubled. But recovery was rapid. By 1999, Korea's GDP growth had rebounded to 10.7%.

### Slide B: Malaysia's Capital Controls — Policy, Evidence For, Evidence Against

**Slide上写的内容（英文，bullet points）：**

- Sept 1, 1998: PM Mahathir imposed capital controls, rejected IMF
- Policy: restricted short-term capital outflows, fixed ringgit at 3.80/USD, lowered interest rates
- Context: Malaysia's private debt ratio higher than Thailand, double South Korea's
- FOR: Kaplan & Rodrik (2001, NBER): time-shifted DiD shows faster recovery, smaller employment loss, faster stock market rebound vs. IMF countries
- AGAINST (macro): Merton Miller (Nobel): controls "at best useless, at worst harmful"; Krugman (1999): countries without controls also recovered
- AGAINST (political economy): Johnson & Mitton (2003, JFE): politically connected firms gained disproportionately; 32% of connected firms' value increase attributable to political connections

**中文讲解：**

马来西亚走了一条完全不同的路。1998年9月1日，马哈蒂尔拒绝了IMF，实施资本管制：限制短期资本外流，把林吉特固定在3.8兑1美元，然后降息刺激经济。背景是马来西亚的私人部门负债率比泰国还高，是韩国的两倍多，所以他认为如果按IMF的方式加息紧缩，马来西亚的金融系统会直接崩溃。

学术界对此的争论非常激烈。支持的一方，Kaplan和Rodrik 2001年在NBER发了一篇很有影响力的工作论文。他们的关键发现是：表面上韩国泰国马来西亚差不多同时恢复，但其实马来西亚实施资本管制的时候危机还在加深，而韩国泰国那时候已经开始恢复了，因为它们接受IMF方案比马来西亚早了好几个月。用time-shifted DiD方法（就是把每个国家的"政策起点"对齐来比较），Kaplan和Rodrik发现马来西亚的复苏更快，就业和工资损失更小，股市反弹也更快。

反对的一方有两层。宏观层面，诺贝尔经济学奖得主Merton Miller直接说资本管制"最好也不过是无用的，最坏是有害的"。Krugman虽然1998年在理论上支持资本管制，但1999年的评估也说，没搞管制的国家也恢复了。政治经济学层面，Johnson和Mitton 2003年发在Journal of Financial Economics上，用firm-level数据发现，跟马哈蒂尔有政治关系的企业在管制实施后涨得特别多，32%的市值增长来自政治连接的价值上升。也就是说，资本管制可能在宏观上有效，但同时也给了裙带资本主义一个保护伞。

**Speaker Note：**

Malaysia took a fundamentally different path. On September 1, 1998, Mahathir imposed capital controls, restricting short-term capital outflows, fixing the ringgit at 3.80 per dollar, and lowering interest rates. His reasoning was that Malaysia's private debt ratio was higher than Thailand's and double Korea's, so IMF-style austerity would collapse the financial system.

The academic debate on this is intense. The most influential supporting study is Kaplan and Rodrik, 2001, NBER Working Paper 8142. Their key insight is about timing. When Malaysia imposed controls in September 1998, its crisis was still deepening, while Korea and Thailand had already begun recovering because they accepted IMF programs months earlier. Using a time-shifted difference-in-differences approach, comparing each country from the start of its policy intervention, they found Malaysia achieved faster recovery, smaller employment and wage losses, and a faster stock market rebound.

On the critical side, there are two layers. At the macro level, Nobel laureate Merton Miller called the controls at best useless and at worst harmful. Krugman, despite theoretically supporting controls in 1998, noted in 1999 that countries without controls also recovered. At the political economy level, Johnson and Mitton, publishing in the Journal of Financial Economics in 2003, found that firms politically connected to Mahathir gained disproportionately after controls were imposed. Approximately 32% of connected firms' value increase was attributable to their political connections. This means capital controls may have simultaneously stabilized the economy and shielded cronyism.

## PART 4: Structural Limits of ELG (3 slides, ~6 min total)

---

### Slide C: The Impossible Trinity — External Demand, Exchange Rates, and Capital Openness

**Slide上写的内容（英文）：**

- The "Impossible Trinity" (Mundell-Fleming): a country cannot simultaneously maintain a fixed exchange rate, free capital movement, and independent monetary policy
- Pre-crisis ELG economies tried to have all three: pegged currencies (for export stability) + open capital accounts (for FDI) + independent monetary policy (for domestic growth)
- When USD appreciated 1995-1997, pegged currencies appreciated passively → exports lost competitiveness
- When speculators attacked, central banks faced a choice: raise interest rates to defend the peg (crushing the domestic economy) or let the currency float (triggering the debt spiral)
- The yen depreciated from ~80/USD to ~120/USD between 1995-1997, squeezing Southeast Asian exporters competing in the same markets
- Japanese banks, dealing with their own bad loans from the bubble era, simultaneously pulled back lending to the region

**中文讲解：**

这是出口导向增长最核心的结构性矛盾。国际经济学里有一个经典理论叫"不可能三角"，也叫Mundell-Fleming三元悖论：一个国家不可能同时维持固定汇率、资本自由流动和独立的货币政策，三者只能取其二。

危机前的东南亚经济体试图三个都要。它们把货币锚定美元来给出口商提供汇率稳定性，同时开放资本账户来吸引外国投资，还想保持独立的货币政策来管理国内经济。这在经济繁荣期看起来没问题，但当外部条件变化时就崩了。

具体来说，1995到1997年美元走强，这些锚定美元的货币跟着被动升值，出口竞争力下降。同一时期，日元从1美元兑80日元左右贬值到120多日元，日本出口变便宜了，直接挤压了东南亚国家在同一市场上的竞争力。而且日本的银行自己还在处理泡沫时代留下的坏账，开始从东南亚撤回贷款。所以东南亚同时面临两个打击：出口市场上被日本挤压，资本来源上被日本银行收缩。

当投机者开始攻击这些货币时，各国央行面临一个两难选择：加息保卫汇率（但这会压垮国内经济），还是放弃固定汇率（但这会引发外债螺旋）。最终大多数国家被迫选择了后者。

**Speaker Note：**

This is the most fundamental structural contradiction of export-led growth. The "impossible trinity" from Mundell-Fleming theory states that a country cannot simultaneously maintain a fixed exchange rate, free capital movement, and independent monetary policy. You can only have two of the three.

Pre-crisis Southeast Asian economies tried to have all three. They pegged their currencies to the dollar for export stability, opened capital accounts to attract investment, and wanted independent monetary policy for domestic management. This worked during the boom, but collapsed when external conditions shifted.

Between 1995 and 1997, the US dollar strengthened, passively appreciating these pegged currencies and eroding their export competitiveness. Simultaneously, the yen depreciated from around 80 per dollar to over 120, making Japanese exports cheaper and squeezing Southeast Asian exporters in the same product markets. Japanese banks, still dealing with bad loans from their own bubble, were also pulling back lending to the region. So Southeast Asia faced a double hit: trade competition from Japan and capital withdrawal by Japanese banks.

When speculators attacked, central banks faced a dilemma: raise interest rates to defend the peg, which would crush the domestic economy, or abandon the peg, which would trigger the foreign debt spiral. Most were forced to choose the latter.

### Slide D: Lack of Domestic Demand

**Slide上写的内容（英文）：**

- ELG model suppresses domestic wages and consumption to maintain export cost competitiveness
- Pre-crisis: household consumption as % of GDP was significantly lower in ELG economies compared to developed countries
- When exports collapsed in 1997-98, there was no domestic demand cushion to absorb the shock
- Contrast with the US: when US exports weaken, domestic consumption (68% of GDP) provides a buffer
- The "adding-up problem": if all developing countries pursue ELG simultaneously, developed countries must run perpetual trade deficits to absorb these exports, which is unsustainable in the long run

**中文讲解：**

出口导向增长的第二个结构性问题是国内需求的缺位。ELG模型为了维持出口成本竞争力，刻意压制工资增长和国内消费。这在经济繁荣期不是问题，因为出口收入在增长。但当出口引擎熄火的时候，国内没有足够的需求来充当缓冲。

对比一下美国：美国家庭消费占GDP的68%左右，即使出口下降，国内消费能撑住经济。但危机前的东南亚经济体，家庭消费占GDP的比重远低于发达国家，国内市场太小，无法在出口崩溃时提供替代性的需求来源。

还有一个更宏观的问题叫"adding-up constraint"（加总约束）：如果所有发展中国家同时追求出口导向增长，那么发达国家就需要持续运行贸易逆差来吸收这些出口。但这在长期是不可持续的。这个问题在1997年的时候还没有完全显现，但它是ELG模型在全球层面的一个根本性限制。

**Speaker Note：**

The second structural limitation is the absence of domestic demand. The ELG model deliberately suppresses wage growth and domestic consumption to maintain export cost competitiveness. During boom times, this is not a problem because export revenues are growing. But when the export engine stalls, there is no domestic demand cushion.

Consider the contrast with the United States, where household consumption represents about 68% of GDP. Even when US exports decline, domestic consumption provides a buffer. In pre-crisis Southeast Asia, household consumption as a share of GDP was significantly lower, and the domestic market was too small to substitute for collapsed export demand.

There is also a broader macro-level issue called the "adding-up constraint." If all developing countries pursue export-led growth simultaneously, developed countries must run perpetual trade deficits to absorb those exports. This is unsustainable in the long run. This problem was not fully visible in 1997, but it represents a fundamental limitation of ELG at the global level.

**建议图表：** 一个柱状图，比较几个国家的household consumption as % of GDP（美国约68%，日本约55%，然后韩国、泰国、马来西亚等危机前的数据，大概在45-55%之间）。视觉上直观展示国内需求缺位。

### Slide E: Post-Crisis Policy Evolution

**Slide上写的内容（英文）：**

- Massive accumulation of foreign exchange reserves (Asia's reserves grew from ~USD 500bn in 1997 to over USD 4 trillion by 2007)
- Shift from fixed pegs to managed floating exchange rate regimes
- Strengthened financial regulation and banking supervision (Basel II adoption, stricter capital adequacy requirements)
- Initial moves toward combining ELG with domestic demand: investment in social safety nets, gradual wage increases, expansion of domestic consumption
- Conclusion: the crisis did not discredit ELG, but revealed that ELG without institutional safeguards (prudent financial regulation, flexible exchange rates, diversified export markets) creates severe systemic fragility

**中文讲解：**

危机之后，这些国家做了几个重大的政策调整，每一个都直接回应了我们刚才讲的结构性问题。

第一，大规模积累外汇储备。亚洲的外汇储备从1997年的大约5000亿美元增长到2007年的超过4万亿美元。这是一个防御性策略：有了足够的储备，就不怕投机者攻击货币。

第二，放弃固定汇率制度，转向管理浮动。这直接解决了不可能三角的矛盾：放弃固定汇率，换取资本开放和货币政策独立性。

第三，加强金融监管，采用巴塞尔协议II的标准，提高银行资本充足率要求。

第四，开始从纯粹的出口导向转向出口加内需并重。投资社会保障网、逐步提高工资水平、扩大国内消费。

所以我们对题目的回答是：1997年的危机没有否定出口导向增长作为发展策略的有效性，过去五十年里没有任何一个国家在不扩大贸易的情况下实现了持续快速增长。但危机证明了，如果没有审慎的金融监管、灵活的汇率制度和出口市场多元化这些制度性保障，ELG模型会产生严重的系统性脆弱性。

**Speaker Note：**

After the crisis, affected countries made significant policy adjustments, each directly addressing the structural vulnerabilities we just discussed.

First, they accumulated massive foreign exchange reserves. Asia's total reserves grew from approximately 500 billion dollars in 1997 to over 4 trillion by 2007. This was a defensive strategy: with sufficient reserves, speculative attacks become much harder to execute.

Second, they shifted from fixed pegs to managed floating exchange rates, directly resolving the impossible trinity by giving up the fixed rate in exchange for capital openness and monetary policy independence.

Third, they strengthened financial regulation, adopting Basel II standards and imposing stricter capital adequacy requirements on banks.

Fourth, they began moving from pure export orientation toward a combination of export-led and domestic demand-led growth, investing in social safety nets, gradually raising wages, and expanding domestic consumption.

So our answer to the title question is this: the 1997 crisis did not discredit export-led growth as a development strategy. No country has achieved sustained rapid growth in the last fifty years without significantly expanding trade. But the crisis demonstrated that ELG without institutional safeguards, meaning prudent financial regulation, flexible exchange rates, and diversified export markets, creates severe systemic fragility.

**建议图表：** 一个时间轴或者带箭头的流程图，展示"危机暴露的问题 → 危机后的政策回应"的对应关系。比如左边写vulnerability（fixed peg），右边写response（managed floating）。

### Slide F: Relating Back to Japan and China

**Slide上写的内容（英文）：**

- Zombie firms: Japan chose forbearance (evergreen lending for 10+ years); Korea was forced by IMF to restructure aggressively (787 institutions closed, Daewoo bankrupt 1999) → Korea recovered faster, illustrating the cost of delayed restructuring
- China mainland: shielded by non-convertible RMB + FDI-dominated capital inflows; committed not to devalue, stabilizing the region
- Hong Kong: fully open currency board, faced full speculative attack; defended peg at 7.80/USD but HSI fell 60%, property halved, GDP -5.5% in 1998
- One country, two exchange rate regimes, two very different crisis experiences

**中文讲解：**

最后简要联系一下我们之前课上讨论的内容。

第一个联系是zombie firms。我们在讲日本失落的十年时学过，日本银行通过evergreen lending维持资不抵债的企业运营，结果zombie firm问题持续了十多年。亚洲金融危机中韩国也面临类似问题，chaebol的负债率极高（大宇3.4倍，现代4.4倍），但区别在于IMF条件迫使韩国选择了激进的出清路径，关闭787家金融机构，让大宇在1999年破产。韩国的恢复速度远快于日本，这说明拖延处理zombie firms的代价是巨大的。

第二个联系是中国。大陆因为人民币不可自由兑换、外资以FDI为主，基本没有受到投机攻击。北京承诺不贬值人民币，被视为区域稳定的锚。但香港完全不同：港币通过联系汇率制度完全锚定美元，资本完全自由流动，所以直接暴露在投机压力下。香港金管局成功保住了7.8的汇率，但恒生指数跌了60%以上，房价腰斩，1998年GDP收缩5.5%。同一个国家，两种汇率制度，两种完全不同的危机体验。这就是汇率制度选择如何直接决定危机脆弱性的最好例子。

**Speaker Note：**

Before we close, let us briefly connect this to previous class topics.

First, zombie firms. When we studied Japan's lost decade, we saw how Japanese banks kept insolvent firms alive through evergreen lending for over a decade. Korea faced a similar problem: chaebol debt levels were extreme, with Daewoo at 3.4 times equity and Hyundai at 4.4 times. But the IMF conditions forced Korea into aggressive restructuring: 787 financial institutions were closed, Daewoo went bankrupt in 1999. Korea's recovery was far faster than Japan's, illustrating the enormous cost of delaying zombie firm resolution.

Second, China. The mainland was largely shielded because the renminbi was not freely convertible and foreign capital entered primarily as FDI rather than short-term flows. Beijing's commitment not to devalue served as a regional stabilizing anchor. Hong Kong, operating under a fully open currency board pegged at 7.80, faced the full force of speculative attack. The HKMA defended the peg successfully, but the cost was severe: the Hang Seng fell over 60%, property values halved, and GDP contracted 5.5% in 1998. One country, two exchange rate regimes, two very different crisis experiences. This is the clearest illustration of how exchange rate regime choices directly determine crisis vulnerability.

## PART 6: Summary (1 slide, ~0.5 min)

### Slide G: Key Takeaways

**Slide上写的内容（英文）：**

1. The 1997 Asian crisis was triggered by a combination of fixed exchange rates, excessive short-term capital inflows, weak financial regulation, and widening current account deficits
2. Policy responses diverged: IMF orthodoxy (Korea, Thailand) imposed short-term pain but enabled fast recovery; Malaysia's capital controls stabilized the economy but also shielded cronyism
3. The crisis revealed three structural limits of ELG: dependence on external demand, the impossible trinity, and the absence of domestic demand as a buffer
4. ELG was not discredited as a strategy, but the crisis proved it requires institutional safeguards to be sustainable

**中文讲解：**

总结四个核心要点。第一，危机的根源是固定汇率、短期资本过度流入、金融监管薄弱和经常账户赤字的组合。第二，政策应对分化：IMF路线短期痛苦大但恢复快，马来西亚资本管制稳定了宏观经济但也庇护了裙带资本主义。第三，危机暴露了出口导向增长的三个结构性局限：外需依赖、不可能三角、国内需求缺位。第四，ELG作为发展策略没有被否定，但危机证明它需要制度性保障才能可持续。

Thank you, happy to take questions.

**Speaker Note：**

To summarize. First, the crisis originated from a combination of fixed exchange rates, excessive short-term capital inflows, weak financial regulation, and widening current account deficits. Second, policy responses diverged: the IMF approach imposed severe short-term costs but enabled rapid recovery, while Malaysia's capital controls stabilized the economy but also shielded cronyism. Third, the crisis exposed three structural limits of export-led growth: dependence on external demand, the impossible trinity, and the absence of domestic demand as a buffer. Fourth, and most importantly, the crisis did not discredit export-led growth as a development strategy, but it proved that ELG requires institutional safeguards to be sustainable.

Thank you. We are happy to take questions.
