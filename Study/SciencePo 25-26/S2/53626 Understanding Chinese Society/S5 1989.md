## A New Social Contract
Pragmatism: sciences, rationalism, ideology. Cadres are engineers of the society
Main objectives: a prosperous, stable and then powerful China
• Improving standards of living
• To make China a great country again
• But without changing the political system
• Gradual reforms, two periods
	1978-1992: prudent approach (East Europe syndrom)
		Introducing capitalist mechanisms and principes
		But the true capitalism in the margins, externalisation of the market
	• And without changing the social system (darwei system, rationing system)
	Erom 1992, more radical reforms: we will see why
	
Main means:
• By introducing certain capitalist practices at the beginning in specific spaces (1980s).
• By imitating a new model of accumulation using foreign capital (Taiwan, Korea, Hong Kong) and relying on exports.
• By relaxing social control (migration, private initiative), change of norms suppose a new way of controlling people: persuasion and self-interest
• Change in values, consumption

No blueprint:
	No experience of reform policy in socialist countries: there are adventurers
	Necessity to be prudent, experimentation as a principle
• Leaders in charge have been dismissed during the cultural revolution (generational effect): politics is dangerous, politics is bad, politics creates troubles
• Role of local cadres: how to involve cadres in promoting economic growth: we need sort of « capitalists » but also bureaucrats
• Role of Asian practices (Role of Hong Kong as a model
- Role but strict control of the Party

## Capitalism in the Margins
### Relaxing of the planning system:
- In Rural areas, local authorities can create collective enterprises (TVEs) , not private but not state osned
- Transactions between the SOEs and the non-state enterprises permitted
-Dual-track system (shaugenishi3& thil) Every single commodity have two prices: a state-planned price and a market price
- SOEs are permitted to set up companies (gongsi 4 5]) dealing exclusively on the market.
- Prices were gradually liberalized
• State-owned enterprises (SOEs): contractual system for hiring people
• Migration, 1984 Rural dwellers were allowed to work in market towns as long as they could earn a living (11 million in 1982. 29 in 1990)
• Private business in urban areas (officially in 1988 but in fact before), petty capitalism. Xiahai movement
• Foreign investment
- 1979 The Law of the People's Republic of China on Chinese-Foreign Equity Joint Ventures,
- 1982 The Constitution was amended to allow the use of foreign capital.
- 1979 The Law of Sino-Foreign Joint Venture was enacted, stipulating that land-use fees should be. charged to foreign enterprises and joint ventures.
• But the economic growth was not able to satisfy new aspirations
