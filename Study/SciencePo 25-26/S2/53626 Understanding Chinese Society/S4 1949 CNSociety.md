# The Chinese society in 1949
• Many peasants (75/80% of labor force) very few farming lands.
	Nearly 10% of families control 60% of lands, 30% own less than two hectares
	Many of them (including the elite) are in a very fragile situation
• Lack of infrastructures, heavy industry underdeveloped, industry only in a very small number of cities (SH 20%)
• Very important role played by the State in the economy but no welfare system: relying on family and personal network
• Life expectancy 35 years, literate rate 30/40% among males and 10% among females but 40% of children go to school.
• Very few workers (2,5 million, 0.5% of the population), a vast majority in a few cities.
In these cities many prostitutes, bandits, refugees
• Very small fringe of wealthy people and of middle classes (petite bourgeoise and new middle class)
• No upward mobility
• Two Chinas: Westernized and non Westernized

## What the CCP want to do in 1949?
Nationalist objective: rebirth and reunification of China. The most important at the beginning
Creation of a new (socialist) society
	• The role of economic development?
	• As the main objective but limited to a model: USSR
• Or focusing on the reshaping the society, economic development in second place or as a by-product?
From 1949 to 1957, the leadership is unified:
	• Cleaning the country, restoring order
	• Industrialisation and bureaucratization: power of the cadres
		Efficiency: necessity to accept compromises with capitalism: piece rates, consumption
		Education: experts and professionals but to which extent
		Health : Western medecine combined with Chinese medecine
		With the help of the Soviet Union

## What happened at the end of the 1950s?
Crisis:
	All resources channeled to heavy industry, light industry very weak
	Great dependance to USSR which put limits to industrialisation (reimbursement in cash and in agricultural goods).
	Break with USSR: where to find money?
	Social troubles: workers (living standard, consumption, hyper control of the CCP)
After 1957 struggle within the Party:
	• Leftist policies Great Leap Forward (1958-1961): radical collectivisation and classe struggle as ways to develop the economy
	• Return to industrialization 1962-1966 (a sort of a moderate open-door policy).
	• Cultural Revolution (1966-1976), once again class struggle
After 1957 totalitarian tendencies
	• New "Chinese man": revolutionary spirit, sacrifice
	• Totalitarian power: only politics, the individual does not exist
	• Equality (in fact a dominant class
	• China isolated: Cold war, difficult relations with the USSR.

## Rural movement mainly but with urban consequences
Migration forbidden: urban population too costly
• China breaks off politically and ideologically with the USSR
• Relying on its own strengths: rural labour force, revolutionary spirit, etc.
• Utopian objectives: catches up with England within 15 years
• An omnipotent political apparatus: limitless power of local cadres
• The notion of "individual" wiped out, including for cadres (no other choice than to succeed)
• Struggle against the bad classes and the tradition
• Abolition of private property in the countryside: Creation of people's communes (10,000/80,000 persons)
→ Autarchy and self-reliance, abolition of trade: rural industry (small furnaces), urban agriculture
• Extreme moblization of people (militarization), especially peasants
• No need of professionals or intellectuals
→ Assessment:
	30/55 million of deaths due to famine, malnutrition and execution (1959-1961), especially in the countryside
	Collapse of farming and of industrial production
	A significant decrease in people's confidence in the CCP
	After that a sort period of recovery (1962-1966)
