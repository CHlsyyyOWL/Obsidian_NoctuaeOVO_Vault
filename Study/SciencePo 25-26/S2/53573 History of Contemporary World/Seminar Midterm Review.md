## 💡 PART 3: INTERNAL ANALYSIS万能模板（第9-35分钟）

### **模板A：用于政治演讲/宣言**

```
INTERNAL ANALYSIS:

1. MAIN ARGUMENT (主要论证):
   The author argues that [ONE clear claim].
   
   Evidence from text:
   - First, [point 1 + quote or paraphrase]
   - Second, [point 2 + quote or paraphrase]
   - Third, [point 3 + quote or paraphrase]

2. WHO IS THE AUDIENCE? (说服对象):
   The author is trying to convince [audience] that [what belief].
   
   Why this matters:
   → If aimed at [internal/external/international audience], it signals...

3. WHAT IS THE AUTHOR RESPONDING TO? (背景反应):
   This document responds to [specific situation/criticism/event].
   
   Context:
   - Before this: [What happened before]
   - The problem: [What author sees as wrong]
   - The solution: [What author proposes]

4. UNDERLYING TENSION (隐含冲突):
   Hidden conflict in this text:
   - On the surface: [What author publicly says]
   - Beneath the surface: [What they're really worried about]
   - Evidence: [Defensive tone / repeated emphasis / careful wording]
```

## 🎯 PART 5: 押题主题讲解 + DATES

### **THEME 1: DECOLONIZATION & ANTI-COLONIALISM**

### **主题1：去殖民化与反殖民主义**

**📅 Key Dates / 关键日期:**

```
1942 → Atlantic Charter (大西洋宪章) - Roosevelt & Churchill宣布人民自决权
1945 → UN Charter established (联合国成立) - 正式承认人民自决
1947 → India independence (印度独立) - 大规模去殖民化开始
1954 → Geneva Accords (日内瓦协议) - Vietnam分裂，殖民地战争
1960-1975 → African decolonization wave (非洲解放浪潮)
1961 → Frantz Fanon "The Wretched of the Earth" published (出版) - 反殖民宣言
```

**🔑 5分钟速读版：**

**英文版：** Decolonization is the process where colonial powers (Britain, France, Portugal, Belgium) lose control of their colonies, especially in Africa and Asia. **Three things drive this:**

1. **War damage** - WWII weakened European powers; they couldn't hold colonies anymore
2. **Ideology shift** - UN Charter (1945) & Atlantic Charter (1942) officially said people have right to self-determine
3. **Resistance** - Anti-colonial movements (nationalist, sometimes communist) fight for independence

**Two different approaches emerge:**

- **Peaceful** - India negotiates with Britain (1947), gets independence
- **Violent** - French-Indochina War (1946-54), Algerian War (1954-62), African liberation wars

**Key thinker: Frantz Fanon** - A psychiatrist from Martinique who writes "The Wretched of the Earth" (1961). His argument: Colonial violence is so brutal that colonized people responding with violence is _justified self-defense_, not aggression. This becomes Bible for liberation movements (Black Panthers, Palestinian groups, etc.)

**Main concept: Negritude** - A movement celebrating Black identity/culture/history as response to colonial erasure. Famous quote by Aimé Césaire: "A civilization that justifies colonization is already sick and deserves its punishment."

---

**中文版：** 去殖民化是指殖民国家（英国、法国、葡萄牙、比利时）失去对殖民地控制的过程，特别是在非洲和亚洲。**三个因素驱动这个过程：**

1. **战争破坏** - 二战削弱了欧洲列强，无法再维持帝国
2. **意识形态转变** - 《联合国宪章》(1945)和《大西洋宪章》(1942)正式确认人民有自决权
3. **抵抗运动** - 反殖民运动（民族主义、共产主义）为独立而战

**两种不同路径：**

- **和平** - 印度与英国谈判(1947)，获得独立
- **暴力** - 法属印度支那战争(1946-54)、阿尔及利亚战争(1954-62)、非洲解放战争

**关键思想家：弗朗茨·法农** - 来自马提尼克的精神科医生，著有《地球上的被压迫者》(1961)。他的观点：殖民暴力极其残暴，被殖民者的反击暴力是_正当防卫_而非侵略。这部书成为解放运动（黑豹党、巴勒斯坦等）的圣经。

**核心概念：黑人性(Negritude)** - 一个庆祝黑人身份/文化/历史的运动，是对殖民地文化抹杀的回应。著名引用，艾梅·塞泽尔：「为殖民化辩护的文明本身已经病了，应该受到惩罚。」

### **THEME 2: COUNTERCULTURE & SOCIAL MOVEMENTS**

### **主题2：反文化与社会运动**

**📅 Key Dates / 关键日期:**

```
1956 → Hungarian Uprising (匈牙利起义) - 东欧首次反抗苏联
1960s → Civil Rights Movement peaks (民权运动高峰)
1963 → Liblice Conference (利布利采会议) - 捷克知识分子开始公开讨论
1967 → Czechoslovak Writers' Conference (捷克作家会议) - Prague Spring前夜
1968 → May 68 in France (法国五月风暴), Prague Spring (布拉格之春)
1968 → First Earth Day proposed (地球日倡议)
1970 → First Earth Day (首个地球日)
```

**🔑 5分钟速读版：**

**英文版：** Counterculture means: **young people rejecting mainstream society's values & creating alternative ways of living.** Starts with beatniks (1950s), explodes in 1960s.

**Why 1960s is different:**

- After WWII → Baby boom generation (born 1946-1965) = huge youth population
- Post-war prosperity → Youth have money, time, freedom unlike parents
- Vietnam War → Young people drafted/dying; they rage against government
- Music shifts → Rock & Roll becomes political (Beatles, Rolling Stones)
- Eastern Europe too → Students in Prague demand freedom, cultural openness

**Social Movement Definition (from Alain Touraine):** A social movement must have THREE things:

1. **Collective interests** - Group pursuing shared goal
2. **Identity reconstruction** - Creating new self-identity (student/activist/rocker)
3. **Challenge to power** - Trying to change rules of game, not just reform

**Key examples:**

- Protests against Vietnam War (students chanting "No!")
- Civil Rights (Black Americans demanding equal rights)
- Prague Spring (1968) - Czech people demand cultural freedom within socialism
- Women's liberation, anti-nuclear movements

**Music & counterculture:**

- Radio stations advertise: "Either you grow with music, or music outgrows you"
- Protests use songs as anthems
- Folk music + Rock become revolutionary tools

---

**中文版：** 反文化(Counterculture)指的是：**年轻人拒绝主流社会价值观、创造替代生活方式的运动。** 始于1950年代的垮掉派，在1960年代爆炸。

**为什么1960年代不同：**

- 二战后 → 婴儿潮一代（1946-1965出生）= 庞大青年人口
- 战后繁荣 → 年轻人有金钱、时间、自由，与父母不同
- 越南战争 → 青年被征兵/战死，他们愤怒反抗政府
- 音乐转变 → 摇滚乐变成政治（披头士、滚石乐队）
- 东欧也一样 → 布拉格学生要求自由、文化开放

**社会运动定义（Alain Touraine）：** 一个社会运动必须有三样东西：

1. **集体利益** - 一群人追求共同目标
2. **身份重构** - 创造新的自我认同（学生/激进分子/摇滚乐手）
3. **挑战权力** - 试图改变游戏规则，不只是改革

**关键例子：**

- 反越南战争抗议（学生喊"不"）
- 民权运动（黑人美国人要求平等权利）
- 布拉格之春(1968) - 捷克人要求社会主义框架内的文化自由
- 女性解放、反核运动

**音乐与反文化：**

- 电台广告："要么你随音乐成长，要么音乐让你落伍"
- 抗议用歌曲作为口号
- 民谣+摇滚乐成为革命工具

### **THEME 3: COLD WAR COOLING & ENVIRONMENTAL HISTORY**

### **主题3：冷战降温与环境历史**

**📅 Key Dates / 关键日期:**

```
1956 → Khrushchev's "Secret Speech" (赫鲁晓夫秘密演讲) - 去斯大林化开始
1962 → Cuban Missile Crisis (古巴导弹危机) - 世界差点核战
1968 → Soviet invasion of Czechoslovakia (苏联入侵捷克) - 冷战升温
1970 → First Earth Day (首个地球日) - 20万美国人参与
1972 → Limits to Growth report (《增长的极限》报告)
1972 → Stockholm UN Environmental Conference (联合国环境会议)
1976 → Berlinguer speech at Soviet Communist Party Congress - Eurocommunism
1979 → Donald Worster "Dust Bowl" (《尘埃碗》出版)
1983 → William Cronon "Changes in the Land" (《土地的改变》出版)
```

**🔑 5分钟速读版：**

**英文版:**

**PART A: Cold War "Cooling" (1970s-80s Détente)**

After Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) = terrifying nuclear near-miss. Both superpowers get scared.

**What changes:**

- US & USSR talk more (diplomacy > direct threat)
- Trade increases (Nixon visits China 1972, breakthrough)
- Arms treaties signed (SALT I 1972 - limiting nuclear weapons)
- BUT: Not real friendship - still competing (proxy wars in Africa, etc.)
- Definition: **Détente** = reduction of Cold War tensions, NOT end of rivalry

**Key moment: 1968 Prague Spring then crushed**

- Czechoslovaks try to have "socialism with a human face" (more freedom)
- Soviet tanks roll in → Brezhnev Doctrine = USSR can invade any satellite state
- Message: Even during "cooling," USSR maintains control through force
- This inspires Eurocommunism → European communists want independence from USSR (Berlinguer in Italy)

---

**PART B: Environmental History Emerges (1960s-70s)**

**What is Environmental History?** New field of history studying: **How humans interact with nature over time.** Not just political/military history - _include non-human world._

**Three research areas:**

1. **Material environment history** - What humans do to nature (deforestation, pollution) & what nature does to humans (climate, disease)
2. **Policy/political history** - Governments' efforts to regulate environment (laws, conservation)
3. **Cultural/intellectual history** - What humans _think_ about nature (philosophy, religion, art)

**Why emerge in 1960s-70s?**

- Rachel Carson "Silent Spring" (1962) warns about pesticides
- First Earth Day 1970 → 20 million Americans participate
- Awareness: Industrial growth = environmental destruction
- Climate consciousness rising
- Decolonization + development → Questions about "progress" & whose cost?

**Key scholars:**

- **Alfred Crosby** (1972) "Columbian Exchange" - How Columbus brought diseases/plants that changed world ecosystems
- **Donald Worster** (1979) "Dust Bowl" - 1930s American ecological disaster caused by capitalism + drought
- **William Cronon** (1983) "Changes in the Land" - How European colonizers transformed New England ecology

**Main insight:** History isn't just about politics - nature shapes history. Humans reshape nature. Understanding past requires understanding both.

---

**中文版：**

**第A部分：冷战"降温"（1970-80年代缓和）**

古巴导弹危机(1962)之后 = 令人恐惧的核战险情。两个超级大国都吓到了。

**什么改变了：**

- 美国和苏联多谈判（外交>直接威胁）
- 贸易增加（尼克松1972年访华，突破性进展）
- 签署军备条约（第一阶段战略武器限制谈判1972）
- 但是：不是真正友谊 - 仍在竞争（非洲代理战争等）
- 定义：**缓和(Détente)** = 冷战紧张降低，不是冷战结束

**关键时刻：1968布拉格之春然后被镇压**

- 捷克人试图"有人道主义面孔的社会主义"（更多自由）
- 苏联坦克进入 → 勃列日涅夫主义 = 苏联可以入侵任何卫星国家
- 信号：即使在"降温"时期，苏联仍通过武力维持控制
- 这激励了欧洲共产主义 → 欧洲共产党要求独立于苏联（意大利贝林格)

---

**第B部分：环境历史兴起（1960-70年代）**

**什么是环境历史？** 历史学新领域研究：**人类随时间如何与自然互动。** 不仅仅是政治/军事历史 - _包含非人类世界_。

**三个研究领域：**

1. **物质环境历史** - 人类对自然的影响（砍伐、污染）以及自然对人类的影响（气候、疾病）
2. **政策/政治历史** - 政府调节环境的努力（法律、保护）
3. **文化/思想历史** - 人类如何_思考_自然（哲学、宗教、艺术）

**为什么在1960-70年代兴起？**

- 蕾切尔·卡森《寂静的春天》(1962)警告杀虫剂危害
- 首个地球日1970 → 2000万美国人参与
- 意识觉醒：工业增长 = 环境破坏
- 气候意识上升
- 去殖民化 + 发展 → 质疑"进步"和谁付代价

**关键学者：**

- **阿尔弗雷德·克罗斯比**(1972)《哥伦布大交换》- 哥伦布带来的疾病/植物如何改变全球生态系统
- **唐纳德·沃斯特**(1979)《尘埃碗》- 1930年代美国生态灾难，由资本主义+干旱引起
- **威廉·克伦**（1983）《土地的改变》- 欧洲殖民者如何改变新英格兰生态

**核心洞察：** 历史不仅关于政治 - 自然塑造历史。人类改造自然。理解过去需要理解两者。

## 🎯 PART 6: 实际演练

### **模拟练习题：政治演讲**

```
[IMAGINE THIS TEXT:]

"The development of our nation depends on protecting the dignity 
of all workers. For too long, the few have controlled the many. 
We demand not just reforms—we demand transformation. 
This is not radical; this is justice."

— A political leader, 1968, speaking at a youth rally

ANSWER THIS:

EXTERNAL ANALYSIS (3 min):
[Your answer]

INTERNAL ANALYSIS (20 min):
- Main argument:
- Audience:
- Historical response to:
- Underlying tension:

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE (5 min):
- Why matters:
- Broader trend:
- Connected to:
- What it proves:
```

**SAMPLE ANSWER:**

```
EXTERNAL ANALYSIS:
This is a political speech by an unidentified leader at a youth gathering 
in 1968. The speaker claims to represent working people, using immediate, 
powerful language ("we demand"). This is direct communication - the speaker 
is present, addressing audience face-to-face, making this high-impact rhetoric.

INTERNAL ANALYSIS:

Main argument: 
The speaker argues that national development requires "dignity for all workers" 
and that incremental reform is insufficient - only "transformation" will work.
Evidence: "For too long, the few have controlled the many" (shows class conflict)
& "not reforms—transformation" (rejects compromise).

Audience:
Directed at youth who feel excluded from power. The speaker wants them to 
believe they have legitimate right to demand complete systemic change, 
not minor improvements. The phrase "This is not radical; this is justice" 
directly addresses youth fear of being labeled "radical" - reframing it 
as moral imperative.

Historical context:
This 1968 speech responds to:
- Vietnam War (youth fighting/dying for government decisions they didn't make)
- Persistent inequality despite 1960s reforms
- Growing militant student movements demanding systemic change
- Global protests (Paris, Prague, Chicago, Mexico City all in 1968)

Underlying tension:
Surface: "We want justice and dignity"
Reality beneath: Author is negotiating delicate position - trying to channel 
youth anger into movement while controlling it. "Not radical" = worried about 
being seen as dangerous.

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE:

Why it matters: 
1968 is peak moment of global youth rebellion. This speech is artifact of that moment,
showing how radical movements frame themselves and mobilize followers.

Broader trend:
This exemplifies shift from 1950s conformity → 1960s challenge to all authority.
Youth no longer accept parents' / government's / institutions' worldview.

Connected to:
- May 1968 Paris student strikes (similar demands for systemic change)
- Prague Spring (same month, youth demanding freedom)
- Democratic Convention protests (Chicago, same year)
- Civil Rights movement (demanding dignity, justice)

What it proves:
Shows that by late 1960s, "radical" demands were becoming mainstream among youth.
The speaker's need to say "this is not radical" proves they understand listeners 
might see it that way - but society is shifting.
```

## 🚀 FINAL TIPS

### **Do This:**

✅ Write in complete sentences (not bullet points alone) ✅ Quote or paraphrase small portions with analysis ✅ Explicitly connect evidence to your argument ✅ Use phrase: "This reveals..." or "This suggests..." or "This proves..." ✅ Remember to contextualize (WHEN matters)

### **Don't Do This:**

❌ Just summarize what text says (that's paraphrasing) ❌ Make up historical facts ❌ Assume author's hidden intentions without text evidence ❌ Use modern vocabulary to judge past people ❌ Write "it was important" without explaining why

### **If You Get Stuck:**

**You don't understand the text?** → Start with: "This document suggests the author believes that [SIMPLE statement]. The evidence is [quote/paraphrase]."

**You don't see the historical significance?** → Ask yourself: "If this document exists, what must be true about that time period? What problem was the author trying to solve?"
