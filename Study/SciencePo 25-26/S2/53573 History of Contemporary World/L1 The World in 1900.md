The 1899 Hague Convention: Limitations and contradictions
- Concerned methods of war (1899 &1907): limits on weapons & tatics
- This followed the earlier 1864 Geneva Convention and the red Cross movement from the 1860s onwards that concerned protections in war
	- - first wounded soldiers, then medical personnel, later different categories of civilians
- Neither set of treaties limited the waging of was: both permitted military necessity and used the principle of proportionality
