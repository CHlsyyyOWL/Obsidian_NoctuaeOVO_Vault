# 演讲稿 · Culture from Nation-Building to Globalization

> **Zygmunt Bauman · *Culture in a Liquid Modern World*, Chapter 3**  
> 2011, Polity Press — 精简版演讲稿（约 10 分钟）

---

## Slide 1 · Culture from Nation-Building to Globalization

*Zygmunt Bauman · Culture in a Liquid Modern World, Chapter 3 · 2011, Polity Press* &emsp; **⏱ 30 秒**
[30 seconds]
Today we examine Zygmunt Bauman's third chapter, 'Culture from Nation-Building to Globalization,' from his 2011 work Culture in a Liquid Modern World. This chapter is pivotal: it shows how globalization has dismantled the nation-state's capacity to maintain cultural unity—and who benefits from the resulting fragmentation.

---

## Slide 2 · Chapter Overview — Five Core Arguments
*全章路线图* &emsp; **⏱ 1 分钟**
### 幻灯片要点

- 1. The Homeostatic System — 文化作为固体现代性民族国家的稳定装置
- 2. Three Phases of Migration — 殖民外移 → 后殖民回流 → 全球离散族群
- 3. Collapse of Cultural Hierarchies — 垂直同化被水平竞争所取代
- 4. Living with Difference — 文化多元作为永久条件，而非暂时摩擦
- 5. Divide & Rule — 全球精英战略性地利用地方碎片化
### 演讲稿全文

[1 minute]

Bauman's chapter works on three interconnected levels. First, he reconstructs the classical role of culture as a homeostatic mechanism in the 'solid' nation-state era. Second, he traces three historical waves of migration that have progressively dissolved that model. Third—and most provocatively—he argues that the resulting cultural fragmentation is not merely a side-effect of globalisation, but is actively exploited by transnational elites through a 'divide and rule' logic.

---

## Slide 3 · Argument 1 — The Homeostatic System

*Solid modernity: culture as social thermostat* &emsp; **⏱ 1.5 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- 在"固体现代性"阶段，民族国家作为自我平衡系统运作，文化是其恒稳装置。
- 文化功能：通过教育、语言政策和同化压力不断纠偏、恢复既定模式。
- 文化元素依其"功能性"（是否稳定系统）来评判，而非其自身价值。
- 全球化彻底摧毁了这一模式：对国家边界与文化内容的掌控能力消失。

### 关键引文

> **[Homeostatic Function]**
>
> "...the impulse to define and judge all elements or aspects of society in terms of their homeostatic qualities and effects was the natural tendency of those societies."

> **[The Transition]**
>
> "For as long as aspirations for a system's monotonous self-recreation lived on (but no longer), the homeostatic vision of culture remained immune to criticism. But aspirations began to weaken, and eventually, under the pressure of globalization, had to be abandoned altogether—reluctantly at first, but soon without major doubts, and indeed willingly."

### 演讲稿全文

[1.5 minutes]

Bauman begins by reconstructing the classical function of culture in the era he calls 'solid modernity'—the nineteenth and early twentieth century period of stable, bounded nation-states. Culture was not primarily about self-expression or aesthetic pleasure: it was a homeostatic mechanism. Like a thermostat, it continuously detected deviations from the national norm—immigrants, dissidents, subcultures—and corrected them through education, language policy, and assimilation pressure.

The key point is functionality: cultural elements were judged by whether they stabilised or destabilised the system. Art, literature, schooling were instruments of social reproduction. This model depended on a crucial assumption—that the state could control its borders, its population, and its cultural content. Globalization shattered that assumption.

---

## Slide 4 · Argument 2 — Three Phases of Global Migration

*From colonial projection to permanent diaspora* &emsp; **⏱ 2 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- Phase 1 (1800s–1940s) · Colonial Emigration — 约6000万欧洲人移居"空旷土地"，强制推行欧洲文化。
- Phase 2 (1940s–1990s) · Post-Colonial Return — 殖民地人口回流宗主国，同化模式反向施用，过渡性框架。
- Phase 3 (1990s–今) · Global Diasporas — 族裔/宗教/语言飞地构成无限群岛，双重或多重忠诚，无单一同化模型可适用。
- 关键推论：文化差异首次在现代史上成为永久条件，而非通往同质性的暂时摩擦。

### 关键引文

> **[Age of Diasporas]**
>
> "The third phase of modern migration...introduces the age of diasporas: an infinite archipelago of ethnic, religious and linguistic settlements, heedless of the pathways marked out and paved by the imperial/colonial episode..."

> **[Permanent Condition]**
>
> "The latest phase of migration poses a question mark over the incipient and unbreakable bond between identity and nationality. For the first time the art of living with difference has become an everyday problem."

### 演讲稿全文

[2 minutes]

Bauman uses migration as the empirical lens through which to trace the dissolution of the homeostatic model.

Phase 1—colonial emigration—extended the nation-state outward. Some 60 million Europeans moved to what were termed 'empty lands,' carrying European culture as a supposedly universal standard. Assimilation meant making others like us.

Phase 2—post-colonial return—reversed the direction. Former colonial subjects moved to the metropolis. The assimilation model was applied again, but with decreasing success and increasing tension.

Phase 3—the current age of diasporas—is qualitatively different. Migration today creates what Bauman calls 'an infinite archipelago of ethnic, religious and linguistic settlements,' heedless of colonial pathways. People maintain dual or multiple loyalties; no country is exclusively sending or receiving. This phase does not fit the assimilation model at all.

The critical implication: for the first time in modern history, cultural difference is not a transitional irritant on the road to homogeneity. It is the permanent condition.

---

## Slide 5 · Argument 3 — Collapse of Cultural Hierarchies

*From vertical assimilation to horizontal contestation* &emsp; **⏱ 1.5 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- 旧模式（垂直等级）：单一演化路径 / 同化即进步 / 欧洲标准为顶点 / 中心-边缘结构 / 文化差异即缺陷。
- 新模式（水平关系）：多元并存路径 / 无预设目标 / 诸文化原则上平等 / 悬浮漂浮 / 争夺"差异许可"。
- 关键区分：水平多元并不产生和谐，而是产生永久性争议——争夺被承认为不同的权利。
- Simmel 的比喻：各文化如同比重相近的粒子，漂浮于文化悬浊液中，无人沉降，无人升起。

### 关键引文

> **[Horizontal Model]**
>
> "The new interpretation breaks down the hierarchy of cultures inherited from the past, and tears apart the model of assimilation as a naturally progressive cultural evolution heading inexorably towards a predetermined model goal. Axiologically speaking, cultural relations are no longer vertical but horizontal: no culture can demand or be entitled to subservience, humility or submission on the part of any other."

> **[Floating Suspension]**
>
> "Ways of life today drift in varied and not necessarily coordinated directions...they do all this (to paraphrase Simmel's memorable phrase) floating in a suspension of cultures, all of a similar, or of a wholly identical specific gravity."

### 演讲稿全文

[1.5 minutes]

The shift from Phase 1 to Phase 3 migration coincides with a fundamental ideological transformation: the collapse of cultural hierarchies.

The old model was explicitly vertical. European civilisation stood at the pinnacle; all others were at lower stages of development. Assimilation was framed as progress—newcomers ascending toward the standard. This justified colonial domination and domestic integration pressure.

The new model is officially horizontal. The multicultural turn asserts that no culture can claim superiority over another. Difference is not deficiency; it is legitimate. Bauman quotes Simmel: cultures today float in suspension, like particles of similar specific gravity—no one sinks, no one rises.

But Bauman's point is not celebratory. The shift from vertical hierarchy to horizontal plurality does not produce harmony. It produces permanent contestation—what he calls the competition for 'permission to be different.' The old certainties are gone, but so is any shared framework for resolving conflicts.

## Slide 6 · Argument 4 — Living with Difference

*Permanent plurality as the unprecedented modern condition* &emsp; **⏱ 1.5 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- Psychological 心理挑战：在无稳定民族/社区锚点的条件下持续协商身份认同。
- Political 政治挑战：国家已失去强制同化或保证文化同质性的工具。
- Ontological 本体论挑战："家"、"归属"与"民族性"等基本现代范畴失去清晰性。
- Rutherford 案例：伦敦某条街，居民文化/语言/习俗各异，边界蜿蜒多孔，无法判断谁在家、谁是外来者。

### 关键引文

> **[Rutherford on Diaspora]**
>
> "The residents of the London street...belong to a neighbourhood of communities widely different from one another in terms of their culture, language and customs...It is a neighbourhood of meandering, spongy and porous boundaries, in which it is difficult to ascertain who legally belongs and who is a stranger, who is at home, and who is an intruder. Where do we belong when we live in a neighbourhood like this one?" — Jonathan Rutherford, cited in Bauman

> **[Here to Stay]**
>
> "The lives of many, perhaps the majority of us Europeans are lived today in diaspora...For the first time the art of living with difference has become an everyday problem...Unlike in the past, the reality of living in close proximity with strangers seems to be here to stay..."

### 演讲稿全文

[1.5 minutes]

Bauman's fourth argument is deceptively simple but philosophically profound: living with difference is now permanent. This is genuinely unprecedented.

In every previous era, cultural difference in the nation-state was understood as temporary. Immigrants would assimilate. Colonial subjects would be 'civilised.' The different would become the same. Now, for the first time, that assumption has collapsed.

Bauman illustrates this through Jonathan Rutherford's description of a London street where residents share a neighbourhood but belong to communities 'widely different in terms of culture, language and customs'—with 'meandering, spongy and porous boundaries' where it is genuinely unclear who is at home and who is a stranger.

This creates three simultaneous challenges: psychologically, people must construct identity without stable national anchors; politically, states have lost the tools to manage this; and ontologically, the basic categories of modern belonging—home, nationality, community—lose their clarity.

---

## Slide 7 · Argument 5 — Global Power & the Divide-and-Rule Strategy

*Cultural fragmentation as strategic outcome, not accidental by-product* &emsp; **⏱ 1.5 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- SPACE OF FLOWS（流动空间）——全球精英：超地域且流动；可逃脱本地后果；受益于碎片化；不受地方政治羁绊。
- SPACE OF LOCALITIES（地方空间）——普通人：地域固定；无法逃脱后果；被维持在碎片化与分裂状态；锁入族裔/宗教冲突。
- 策略逻辑：只要底层人群忙于相互争斗，就无法形成威胁全球资本的统一政治反对力量。
- 理论来源：Bauman 援引 Castells 的流动空间 / 地方空间理论作为结构基础。

### 关键引文

> **[Rorty on Elite Strategy]**
>
> "The aim will be to keep the minds of the proles elsewhere—to keep the bottom 75 percent of Americans and the bottom 95 percent of the world's population busy with ethnic and religious hostilities, and with debates about sexual mores. If the proles can be distracted from their own despair by media-created pseudo-events, including the occasional brief and bloody wars, the super rich will have little to fear." — Richard Rorty, cited by Bauman

> **[Space Asymmetry]**
>
> "The ease of movement of the elite in the planetary space of flows...depends to a great extent on the inability or unwillingness of the natives...to act with solidarity." — Bauman

### 演讲稿全文

[1.5 minutes]

Bauman's most provocative argument: cultural fragmentation is not merely a by-product of globalisation—it is strategically useful to those who profit from it.

Bauman draws on Manuel Castells' concept of the 'space of flows' versus the 'space of localities.' Global elites—financial institutions, transnational corporations, digital platforms—operate in the space of flows. They are genuinely exterritorial: their capital moves instantly across borders; their decisions have no fixed location; they can escape the consequences of their choices by simply moving elsewhere.

Ordinary people remain locked in the space of localities. They cannot move their lives with the same ease. They bear the consequences—unemployment, environmental damage, housing crises—locally.

The strategy is this: as long as these ordinary people are kept busy fighting each other—along ethnic, religious, or linguistic lines—they cannot form the unified political opposition that would actually threaten global capital. Bauman cites Richard Rorty's chillingly blunt formulation: keep the 'proles' occupied with ethnic and religious hostilities and media-created pseudo-events, and the super-rich have little to fear.

## Slide 8 · Key Textual Evidence — Three Central Passages

*引文精华* &emsp; **⏱ 1 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- 第一条：确立规范性转变——文化关系官方上已成为水平的，各文化间无价值等级。
- 第二条：记录经验含义——离散族群是永久的，民族国家通过同化管理认同的能力已消失。
- 第三条（最具批判性）：揭示多元文化主义的意识形态功能——将物质贫困重新框架为文化多样性。

### 关键引文

> **[On Horizontal Culture]**
>
> "Axiologically speaking, cultural relations are no longer vertical but horizontal: no culture can demand or be entitled to subservience, humility or submission on the part of any other."

> **[On Permanent Diaspora]**
>
> "The latest phase of migration poses a question mark over the incipient and unbreakable bond between identity and nationality. For the first time the art of living with difference has become an everyday problem."

> **[On Multiculturalism's Paradox]**
>
> "Through this linguistic measure, the moral ugliness of poverty magically turns into the aesthetic appeal of cultural diversity." — Bauman

### 演讲稿全文

[1 minute]

These three passages distil Bauman's three pivotal moves in the chapter.

The first establishes the normative shift: culture is now officially horizontal—no hierarchy of value between cultures. The second registers what this shift means empirically: diaspora is now permanent, and the nation-state's capacity to manage identity through assimilation is gone. The third—and most critical—exposes the ideological function of multiculturalism: by celebrating diversity as an aesthetic value, it reframes material inequality as cultural difference, making poverty aesthetically appealing and politically invisible.

## Slide 9 · Conclusion — The Central Paradox of Chapter 3

*文化平等如何与经济不平等共谋* &emsp; **⏱ 1 分钟**

### 幻灯片要点

- 官方意识形态颂扬文化平等与多样性。
- 结构现实利用这种碎片化来维持经济支配。
- 民族国家已失去维持文化同质性的能力。
- 三波移民浪潮创造了不可逆转的离散族群现实。
- 文化等级崩塌——但被永久碎片化取代，而非和谐。
- 全球精英战略性地培育族裔和宗教分裂以维持权力。
- Bauman 的隐含论点：文化平等与经济不平等相互共谋。

### 关键引文

> **[Implicit Thesis]**
>
> "Cultural equality becomes complicit with economic inequality." — Bauman's implicit thesis

### 演讲稿全文

[1 minute]

Bauman's chapter leaves us with a profound and uncomfortable paradox.

On the surface, everything has improved: we have moved from racial hierarchy and forced assimilation to cultural equality, multiculturalism, and the celebration of diversity. The old vertical model of European superiority is officially dead.

But underneath, the structural conditions for domination have not disappeared—they have been reorganised. The fragmentation of working populations along ethnic and religious lines, the rebranding of material poverty as cultural diversity, and the strategic exploitation of local conflict by exterritorial global elites suggest that cultural equality and economic inequality are not in tension—they are mutually reinforcing.

Bauman forces us to ask: is multiculturalism a genuine emancipatory achievement, or the ideological cover story for the liquidation of the welfare state and the consolidation of global capital?
