# A Rational Theory of the Size of Government

The principle reason for the increased size of the government, measured by the share of income distributed, is implied by the model that the extension of franchise change the position of decisive voters in the income distribution and changed the in relative productivity, Thus leading to increase in mean income relative to the income of decisive voter.

- Voters rationally anticipate the disincentive effects of taxation on the labor-leisure choice of their fellow citizens and take into account when voting.
- The Share earned income redistributed depend on the voting rule and on the distribution of productivity in the economy.
- Under the majority rule, the equilibrium tax share balances the budget and pays for the voters’ choice.
