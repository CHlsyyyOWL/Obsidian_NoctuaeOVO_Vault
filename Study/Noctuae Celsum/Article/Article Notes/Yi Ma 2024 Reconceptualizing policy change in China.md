## The Eight Indicators: Definitions and Scoring

The study measures the **Obligation** (legal/administrative weight) and **Enforcement** (mechanisms ensuring implementation) of soft law policies using four indicators per dimension.

**Dimension 1: Indicators for Obligation**

This dimension measures the "hardness" of the policy's content and its source authority.

| Indicator                     | Definition                                                 | Scoring Criteria                                                                                                                                    |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Document Issuer**           | The administrative level of the body issuing the document. | **+1**: Issuer is above the **General Office** (_bangongting_) level. <br> **-1**: Issuer is at or below the General Office level.                  |
| **Target Specificity**        | The clarity and quantifiability of policy goals.           | **+2**: Contains **numerical targets** (e.g., specific urbanization rates). <br> **-2**: Contains only vague goals (e.g., "substantial increase").  |
| **Instrument Specificity**    | The precision of the policy tools used to achieve goals.   | **+1**: Detailed criteria (e.g., clear employment/housing years required for _hukou_). <br> **-1**: Only vague or general suggestions for transfer. |
| **Supplementary Instruments** | Presence of supporting policies (_peitao cuoshi_).         | **+1**: Detailed support in healthcare, pension, or education. <br> **-1**: No supplementary policy instruments mentioned.                          |

**Dimension 2: Indicators for Enforcement**

This dimension measures the political pressure and monitoring mechanisms attached to the policy.

|Indicator|Definition|Scoring Criteria|
|---|---|---|
|**Inspection**|Use of oversight teams to monitor implementation.|**+1**: Specific inspection or follow-up assessment mechanisms. <br> **-1**: No mention of an inspection mechanism.|
|**Target Responsibility System (TRS)**|Integration into the performance management system.|**+2**: TRS is explicitly specified as a tool for implementation. <br> **-2**: No mention of TRS.|
|**Leadership Group**|Institutional arrangements to coordinate implementation.|**+1**: Establishing or reorganizing a cross-departmental group. <br> **-1**: No leadership or coordination group mentioned.|
|**Public Monitoring**|Transparency and bottom-up supervision requirements.|**+1**: Requirement to disclose documents for public/social supervision. <br> **-1**: No requirement for public disclosure.|
