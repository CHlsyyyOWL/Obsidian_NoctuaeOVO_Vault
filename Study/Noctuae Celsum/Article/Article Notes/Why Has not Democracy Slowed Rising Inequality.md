# Motivation

Democracy existed with the fact that US inequality is rising: why democracy is not slowing inequality?

# Literature review

## Canonical Model (Meltzer and Richard 1981):

increased inequality (measured by the falling median income relate to average income) should cause less inequality, since people want more distribution, and democracy (measured by one person one vote) should respond to it by higher taxes for redistribution. However, this was not true when it comes to high rate of taxes reduced the labor supply.

## Stripped Down version of Canonical Model (Bolton and Roland 1999)

Similar implications that redistribution is limited through the dead weight loss in taxation.

These literature assume Politics is:

- majoritarian
    
- equal (one people one vote)
    
- With full participation
    
## Benabou 2000& McCarty, Poole, and Rosenthal 2006

If redistribution gives efficiency gains, then there is more likely to be redistribution for low inequality states than the higher states.

When inequality level is low, then efficiency gain offset the dead-weight loss; if inequality is high, the rich turned against redistribution.

In any economy, unequal political power can make the rich the pivot voter under majority rule.

Thus, if a society has many poor residents without voting rights (substantial immigration), redistribution will be limited.
