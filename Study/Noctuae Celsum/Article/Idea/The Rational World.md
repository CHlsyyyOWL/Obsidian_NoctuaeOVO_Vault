# Title: The Rational World..？

# Abstract:

With people around the world all becoming obsessed with the US-China war , the Pacific Ocean seems not enough to embrace two super nations. The most common views arising from past decades, more or less, are all explaining from an economic perspective—trade war, supply chain, comparison in gdp growth…plus a bit of geopolitics, like military and national sovereignty, which one could possibly admit that these were largely influenced by its economic performance—-developing military power has been always undoubtedly necessary for a nation developing, thus the only reason restricting the country from strong army is money. It seems obvious that, to best serve for your country in the 21st century, the only skills a diplomat need would be an economic degree (and diplomatic strategy, however becoming increasingly useless these days as we hardly witness any diplomatic success other than reaching agreements with developing asian countries by promising the amount of money that they cannot refuse).

Is this the case，In the world after the WWII and cold war, do people still believe in modern economics, a academic field that with a core assumption of rationality, utility maximization and over simplified model to analyze the world with data and regression with a solo faith in realism, negating the nature of the world that society exists through people’s interactions?

[[S1 Introduction to Social Research]]

In the book of “the clash of civilizations “, the post cold war world was described as a world of cultural conflict, with the rise of economic globalization （multitarinism）bringing the identity crises to the front which seems to echo with this theory and reflect a potential west - east civilization conflict.  Yes，people would say the identity crisis，as well as the anti- globalization（polarization）trend represented by trump‘s policy，were all resulted in this special economic downturn period——again，it come backs to a economic problem. However，you would find economic explanation do not always work in this situation，where such trend was not commonly seen during other economic downturn era and is believed to have a more general and convicing theory unfolded. 

In the book of “the end of an economic man：the origin of totalitarianism” [[经济人的末日|The end of economic man —— the origin of totalitarianism]]，the author re- construct the theory of facism into a inevitable phenomenon that has nothing todo with german’s own special characteristics，but a more generalized desperate in the capitalism and socialism‘s rebellion of the western civil faith of freedom and equality，which，is not an economic problem caused by somewhat depression，but a conflict in the western culture versus the reality. And now，we are again standing on a similar point. 

the world is not rational，and could not be simply understood through economic problem，but a from a more ontological perspective.

1. 逻辑链条不够清晰：段落之间的衔接比较突然，观点有时像是跳跃而来（例如从“经济学的假设”到“文明冲突”）。

二、内容与结构上的整理建议

 
我帮你把你的思路拆解成更有逻辑感的三部分：


1. 引入问题：经济视角的局限


- 世界对中美冲突的解读大多依赖经济学（GDP、贸易战、军事与经济实力挂钩）。
- 这种视角隐含一种假设：一切政治、外交乃至冲突的根源，都归因于经济。
- 你质疑：在后冷战世界，这种解释是否足够？

  
👉 建议：这里可以单独成一段，语句简洁，点明问题。


2. 理论交锋：经济解释 vs 文明/文化解释


- Huntington：《文明的冲突》：后冷战冲突是文化和身份层面的，而不是单纯经济。
- 反全球化与身份危机（如特朗普）——并非所有经济衰退时期都会出现类似现象，因此不能单纯用经济解释。
- 这里可以提出你的判断：文明和文化冲突可能是更“根源性”的变量。

  
👉 建议：这里要更明确区分“经济 downturn 的特殊性”与“文明冲突的普遍性”。


3. 哲学深化：超越经济理性主义


- Drucker：《经济人的终结》：极权主义并非单一的经济问题，而是文化与价值体系的危机。
- 今天世界再一次面临相似困境：理性、功利和简化模型无法解释社会复杂性。
- 结论：理解世界必须回到更本源的社会存在（ontology），而不是停留在工具性的经济模型。

  
👉 建议：结尾处可以提出一个更具体的研究问题，例如：

- “经济学模型的理性假设如何遮蔽了身份、文明与政治的复杂互动？”
- “为什么当代国际秩序的危机需要跨越经济学与社会学的整合视角？”

- 长句拆分：例如第一段开头“一口气三四个从句”，容易让人迷失。建议拆成短句，保留核心节奏。
